# AMD Q1 2026 — extracted

## Overall financials

- **Q1 net revenue: $10.3B** (+38% YoY — RECORD)
- **Operating income: $1.5B** (vs $806M YA)
- **Net income: $1.4B** (vs $709M YA — nearly doubled)

## Data Center segment (the AI story)

- **Data Center revenue: $5.8B** (+57% YoY) — primary engine
- Driven by:
  - **5th gen AMD EPYC processors** (server CPUs)
  - **AMD Instinct MI350 series GPUs** (AI accelerators)

## MI355X performance

- MLPerf results: strong competitive performance across full suite
- Leadership results in multiple categories
- Direct competitor to NVIDIA H100/H200/Blackwell in inference benchmarks

## The Meta deal (major)

- **Meta plans to deploy up to 6 GW of AMD Instinct GPUs**
- **First 1-GW deployment powered by custom AMD Instinct MI450**
- = AMD landed a major hyperscaler custom-silicon win on Instinct family
- Comparable in scope to Broadcom-Google or Broadcom-Meta MTIA deals

## Helios

- Initial volume Q3 2026; significant ramp Q4 → Q1 2027
- Helios = AMD's rack-scale AI factory reference design

## Forward roadmap

- **MI400 series** — Helios reference platform
- **MI450 custom** — Meta deal anchor
- AMD forecasts **$120B server CPU income by 2030**

## Lisa Su commentary

> "A clear inflection in our growth trajectory and a structural shift in our business"

> "As AI adoption scales, demand is increasing not only for accelerators, but also for the high-performance CPUs that power and orchestrate those workloads."

## Strategic context

- AMD = #2 in AI accelerators after NVIDIA (Cerebras + Groq are SRAM-machine alternatives at niche scale)
- 5th gen EPYC = strong server CPU position taking share from Intel Xeon
- MI355X / MI400 / MI450 roadmap = direct alternative to NVIDIA Blackwell/Rubin
