# TSMC Q1 2026 Earnings — extracted call data

## Headline financials

- **Q1 2026 revenue: $35.9 billion** (+6.4% sequential)
- HPC revenue +20% sequential, now **61% of total revenue**
- **Full-year 2026 revenue growth guidance: above 30%** (raised guidance)
- **Q1 2026 capex guidance: $52–56 billion** (raised to high end of range)
- Operating margins: record territory; specifics not in summary

## CoWoS capacity (CRITICAL — direct C.C. Wei quote)

> C.C. Wei: "Our CoWoS capacity is very tight and remains sold out through 2025 and into 2026"

- **CoWoS demand exceeds supply by 40–50%**
- **CoWoS lead times: 50+ weeks** (per Silicon Analysts foundry-allocation tracker)
- Supply constraints expected to persist into 2027

## AI wafer demand

- **11× growth in AI wafer demand 2022–2026** per TSMC's own forecast
- Advanced-node wafer demand "about three times" greater than available supply

## Foundry allocation status (Q1 2026, per Silicon Analysts)

- N3 (3nm): fully booked
- N7: available
- CoWoS: 50+ weeks lead time

## CoWoS roadmap (per TrendForce / TSMC announcements)

- 14-reticle CoWoS version supporting **20 HBM stacks: 2028**
- Beyond 14 reticles supporting **24 HBM stacks: 2029**

## Capex / capacity expansion

- TSMC raised capex to high end of $52-56B range
- Cited "extremely robust" AI demand as the driver
- TSMC capex growth has been accelerating since FY2024 to support AI infrastructure

## Strategic implications

- This source verifies and updates the figures in [[Can AI scaling continue through 2030 (2024)|Epoch 2024]]:
  - Epoch had CoWoS at 33–35K wafers/mo by end-2024 with planned 60%/yr growth — TSMC's actual demand is even higher
  - CoWoS bottleneck is more acute in 2026 than Epoch's projection suggested
  - $52-56B FY2026 capex is roughly 2x the historical capex run-rate
