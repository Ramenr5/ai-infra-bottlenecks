## Key commentary

### CEO Gary Dickerson — the headline call

> "Global semiconductor industry revenues could reach **$1 trillion in 2026**, several years earlier than previously predicted."

This is **a major upgrade to the industry growth trajectory** — most prior consensus had $1T in 2028-2030 window.

### 2026 outlook for AMAT itself

- **>20% growth in semiconductor business in 2026**
- Tracking higher levels of planned capex from **cloud service providers** (i.e. hyperscaler capex absorption confirmed at equipment level)

### Key growth markets cited

1. Leading-edge logic
2. High-bandwidth memory DRAM (HBM)
3. Advanced packaging (CoWoS-adjacent)

## Why it matters

- AMAT is a major Wafer Fab Equipment (WFE) supplier — their order book is a **leading indicator of foundry capex** 6-12 months ahead of fab buildout
- Record DRAM revenue = direct confirmation of HBM-driven DRAM line expansion (validates [[HBM allocation]] supply-side story)
- "$1T industry in 2026" is a primary anchor for [[Capex trajectory thesis]] — supports the acceleration view materially
- Picks-and-shovels exposure to every fab buildout (TSMC, Samsung, Intel, SK Hynix, Micron, plus Chinese fabs subject to export controls)

## Notes

- AMAT competes with **Lam Research (LRCX)** and **KLA Corporation (KLAC)** in the WFE oligopoly
