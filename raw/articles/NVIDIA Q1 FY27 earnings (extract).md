# NVIDIA Q1 FY27 Earnings — extracted call data

## Financials

- **Data Center revenue:** $75B (+92% YoY, +21% sequential)
  - Data Center Computing: $60B (+77% YoY)
  - Data Center Networking: $15B (nearly tripled YoY)
- **Hyperscale segment:** $38B (~50% of data center), +12% sequential
- **Sovereign revenue:** +80% YoY
- **GAAP gross margin:** 74.9%
- **Non-GAAP gross margin:** 75%
- **Q2 FY27 guide:** $91B ± 2% total revenue (sequential growth driven primarily by data center)

## Supply commitments (CoWoS/HBM anchor)

> Colette Kress (CFO): "We remain front footed in securing sufficient supply to support our customers' growth. In Q1, we increased total supply inclusive of inventory purchase commitments on prepaids to **$145 billion**."

## Blackwell deployment

> Jensen Huang: "Our Blackwell architecture is everywhere. Adopted, and deployed by every major hyperscaler every cloud provider, and every major model maker."

Named deployments:
- OpenAI: GPT 5.5 "codesigned for, trained with, and served on Blackwell"
- Microsoft Farweave: "world's most powerful AI data center is now live...powered by hundreds of thousands of Blackwell GPUs"
- AWS: "will add more than 1 million Blackwell and Rubin GPUs"
- Google: "Blackwell will be offered to customers in the cloud"
- Anthropic: new partnership for capacity expansion

## Vera Rubin

- Production shipments commence Q3 FY27 (second half of calendar 2026)
- Architecture: "7 purpose built chips across 5 accelerated racks"
- "35× higher inference throughput and up to 10× greater AI factory revenue compared with Blackwell"
- Vera CPU: "1.5× faster performance per core, 2× performance per watt, and 4× density per rack compared to x86 based alternatives"

## $1 trillion Blackwell+Rubin guidance

> Jensen: "giving us full confidence in **$1 trillion in Blackwell and Rubin revenue** we foresee from 2025 through calendar 2027"

Excludes: standalone Vera CPUs (~$20B anticipated), LPX, CPX products

## Sovereign / international

- "NVIDIA AI infrastructure is now deployed across nearly 40 countries representing $50 trillion in GDP"
- Sovereign included as part of new "ACIE" segment

## China

> "the US government has approved licenses for H200 to be shipped to China based customers, We have yet to generate any revenue. And we are uncertain whether any imports will be allowed into the country."
> "we are not including any China data center compute revenue in our outlook"

## Inference market

> Jensen: "We capitalized on the inflection and inference demand"
- "H100 rental prices increased 20% year to date"
- "A100 cloud pricing up nearly 15%"
- "Every single frontier model company will jump on VeraRubin from the get go"

## Hyperscaler specifics

- AWS: 1M+ Blackwell + Rubin GPUs over a year; collaboration on Spectrum networking
- Google: XGS bare-metal instances supporting "up to 960 thousand Rubin GPUs across multiple sites"
- Microsoft Farweave: hundreds of thousands of Blackwell GPUs operational
