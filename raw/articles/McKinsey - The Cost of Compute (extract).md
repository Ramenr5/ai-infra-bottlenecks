# The Cost of Compute — McKinsey

**Headline figures:**

- **$5.2 trillion** capex for AI-related data centers globally through 2030 (base scenario)
- **$1.5 trillion** capex for traditional IT data centers through 2030
- **$6.7 trillion total** data center capex through 2030
- **$7.9 trillion** in the accelerated-growth scenario

**Capacity additions:**

- **156 GW** total AI-related data center capacity demand by 2030
- **125 GW** incremental AI capacity added between 2025 and 2030 (base scenario)
- **205 GW** incremental AI capacity in the accelerated scenario

**Per-GW implied cost:**
- Base scenario: $5.2T / 125 GW incremental ≈ **$42M per MW** of new AI DC capacity (all-in: power, real estate, chips, MEP, networking)
- Per the Data Centre Magazine summary, this is "based on a projected 156 gigawatts of AI-related data center capacity demand by 2030"

**Methodology notes:**
- Demand-driven model: starts with token-generation/training compute demand, derives required capacity, prices by category
- Two scenarios: "rising" (base, $6.7T) and "accelerated"  ($7.9T)
- Includes chips, real estate/shell, mechanical/electrical/cooling, power infrastructure, networking

**Skeptical voices captured in third-party coverage (IT Brew, May 2025):**
- Some analysts question whether the $7T scenario is achievable given power supply, chip manufacturing, and capital constraints
- ROI assumptions for hyperscalers vs. neoclouds vary widely
- Per DCD: "AI could drive $6.7 trillion investment in data centers, maybe, claims McKinsey"

**Notes:**
- McKinsey is the second major tier-1 analyst voice (alongside Goldman Sachs) on the AI infrastructure capex story
- Often cited by hyperscaler IR teams as a TAM anchor
- Full report has detailed breakdown by region and category — gated on McKinsey.com but figures widely re-reported
