# SK Hynix Q3 2025 Earnings — extracted

## Q3 2025 financials (official SK Hynix release)

- **Revenue: ₩24,448.9 billion** (~₩24.45 trillion won, ~$17B USD at then-spot)
- **Operating profit: ₩11,383.4 billion** (~₩11.38 trillion won)
- **Operating margin: 47%**
- **Revenue +39% YoY**
- **Operating profit +62% YoY**

## HBM commentary (Q3 2025 release)

- Strong HBM and high-performance server product sales drove performance
- "Company has already secured full customer demand for its entire DRAM and NAND production for next year" (i.e., 2026 sold out as of October 2025)
- HBM4 mass production began September 2025
- HBM4 shipments begin Q4 2025
- Full-scale HBM4 sales expansion planned for 2026

## Capex

- "Investment for next year is expected to increase compared to this year"
- M15X facility expansion is the primary vehicle
- Specific 2026 capex amounts not disclosed in Q3 2025 release

## Subsequent-quarter indicators (search coverage)

Per TrendForce / KED Global / Notebookcheck coverage of Q4 2025 / Q1 2026:

- **HBM, DRAM, NAND all sold out for 2026** (multiple confirmations)
- **HBM supply expected to remain tight into 2027**
- **NVIDIA HBM4 allocation: ~2/3 (~70%) to SK Hynix** (Samsung targeting earlier delivery for the rest)
- Per KED Global Q1 2026 indicator (not yet in official SK Hynix release): operating margin expanded further, 72% operating margin cited for the early-2026 quarter
- Per CNBC October 2025 coverage: "SK Hynix, a critical Nvidia supplier, has already sold out chips for 2026 as AI demand booms"

## Strategic position

- SK Hynix is THE dominant HBM supplier; first to HBM3 / HBM3E / HBM4
- Samsung qualification issues with NVIDIA leave SK Hynix even more concentrated as NVIDIA's HBM source
- Micron is third-source, aggressive ramp but smaller share
