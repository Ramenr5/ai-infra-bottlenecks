# EDA Market Analysis: Key Data Points

## Market Sizing & TAM
- Current Market: $18B (2025)
- Expanded TAM: $28-31B by 2030 ($18B EDA+IP + $10B simulation + $3B systems software)
- Growth Rate: 13% CAGR (EDA) vs. 7% (semiconductor R&D)

## Big-3 Revenue & Share
- Synopsys: $8B CY2025 (including Ansys); 85%+ combined market share
- Cadence: $5.30B
- Siemens EDA: $2.2-2.5B (estimated)
- Combined: ~$16B across tools, IP, emulation, simulation

## Tool-Level Market Share (Advanced Nodes, 7nm+)
- Synthesis: Synopsys Design Compiler 84-85%
- Place & Route: Cadence Innovus 50-55%; Synopsys ICC2/Fusion 40-45%
- Signoff Timing: Synopsys PrimeTime 90%+
- Physical Verification: Siemens Calibre 85%+
- Simulation (Functional): Synopsys VCS 45-50%; Cadence Xcelium 40-45%
- Emulation: Cadence Palladium 55-60%; Synopsys ZeBu 35-40%

## Customer Segments & Spending
- Fabless (NVIDIA, Qualcomm, AMD): $80-150K per engineer annually
- IDMs (Intel, TI): $40-80K per engineer
- System companies (hyperscalers, Apple, Tesla): 45% of EDA demand per Cadence
- Turnkey ASIC houses (Broadcom ASIC Group): $200-500M annually estimated

## IP Business Scale
- Synopsys IP: $1.7B
- Cadence IP: $0.7B+
- Total EDA vendor IP: $3B+ market

## Customer Concentration
- Intel (Synopsys largest customer): 12.6% of revenue (FY2024, $772M); below 10% in FY2025
- Synopsys backlog: $11.4B (1.6 years of revenue)
- Cadence backlog: $7.8B (1.5 years of revenue)
- Customer retention: 95%+ annually for core tools; 99%+ for signoff/analog

## Licensing Model Economics
- Contractual escalators: 3-7% annually
- AI tool renewal uplift: ~20% revenue premium over baseline
- Time-based licenses: 70-83% of revenue (Synopsys/Cadence)
- Token consumption: AI features increase usage 3-5x per design project

## Design Costs (Advanced Nodes)
- NVIDIA Blackwell: $100M+ (paywalled)
- Apple M-series/A-series: $170-260M (paywalled)
- 3nm node tools: 3-5x cost vs. 28nm
- Design time: 12-24 months for 7nm/5nm/3nm

## AI Accelerator Opportunity
- Hyperscaler custom silicon: $15-20B new chip design activity (past 5 years)
- Cerebrus AI tapeouts (Cadence): 180 to 1,000+ in 8 quarters

## Central Thesis
EDA market operates as a durable oligopoly with 85%+ Big-3 control, driven by architectural lock-in across sequential tool flows, bundled licensing agreements that obscure per-tool ROI, and foundry mandates that enforce tool selection. Revenue growth (13% CAGR) outpaces semiconductor R&D (7% CAGR) through verification intensity expansion, AI tool premiums (15-25%), and node-transition pricing power—not headcount growth. Customer retention exceeds 95% annually, enabling contractual escalators and AI-driven token consumption to compound without net-new seat sales.
