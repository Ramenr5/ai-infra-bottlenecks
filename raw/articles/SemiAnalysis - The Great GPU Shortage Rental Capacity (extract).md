# GPU Rental Market Data Extraction

## H100 Rental Pricing ($/hour/GPU)
- October 2025: $1.70/hr/GPU
- March 2026: $2.35/hr/GPU
- Change: "almost 40%" increase over 5 months
- Late January 2026: Broke above $2.00/hr/GPU for 1-year contracts
- Mid-to-late February: 15-20% increase vs. end January
- End of March projection: Additional 15-20% month-on-month rise

## Named GPU Rental Providers
- Lambda Labs (on-demand pricing shown)
- Runpod (spot/on-demand focus)
- CoreWeave (public; share price concerns noted)
- Nebius (public)
- IREN (public)

Hyperscalers mentioned but unnamed specifically in rental context.

## Supply/Demand Dynamics

Capacity Status:
- "On-Demand GPU rental capacity is sold out across all GPU types"
- "Half the providers we asked were completely sold out"
- "All capacity coming online until August to September 2026 has already been booked"
- Blackwell lead times: "extending into June-July"

Demand Drivers:
- Claude Code adoption (projected 20%+ of daily commits by end-2026)
- Multi-agent workflows with "parabolic growth in token and compute consumption"
- Media generation (Seedance, Nano Banana)
- Open-weight model adoption

## Cluster Sizing Trends
Long-term offtake deals involve clusters of "50MW or 100MW or even larger – equivalent to about ~24,000 to 48,000 GB300 NVL72 GPUs"

## Market Structure Segmentation
1. Short-term: On-demand, spot, <3 months
2. Mid-term: 3 months to 3+ years (1-year most sensitive indicator)
3. Long-term: 4-5 year offtakes (majority volume by value)

## Central Thesis
"GPU rental pricing is more likely to continue rising than falling" driven by sustained compute scarcity, expanding ROIC margins, extended equipment useful lives, and inelastic demand from high-ROI AI tool adoption.
