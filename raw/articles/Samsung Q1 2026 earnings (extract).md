# Samsung Electronics Q1 2026 — extracted

## Overall financials (extraordinary)

- **Revenue: 133.9 trillion won (~$100B)**
- **Operating profit: 57.2 trillion won (+756% YoY)** — RECORD for any Korean company; **surpassed full-year 2025 operating profit in one quarter**
- All-time high

## Memory Division

- **Revenue: 53.7 trillion won**
- **Memory operating profit alone: ~$33.6B** (per BigGo)
- **Memory chip sales: +292% YoY**
- **HBM revenue: more than tripled YoY**
- **Samsung became the FIRST to ship HBM4 chips AT SCALE for NVIDIA Vera Rubin platform**

### Memory pricing

- **DRAM contract prices: +90-95% QoQ in Q1 2026**
- **NAND flash prices: +55-60% QoQ in Q1 2026**

## Foundry Business

- Earnings declined Q1 (off-peak seasonality)
- Q2 plan: **full utilization of advanced-node production lines**
- Q2 target: earnings improvement on increased HBM4 base-die supply
- **SF2 yields: ~20% late 2025 → 55-60% early 2026** (per TrendForce)
- **SF2X (HPC) 2026; SF2A (automotive) + SF2Z (BSPDN) 2027**
- Tesla long-term agreement: **$16.5B**
- AI customer wins: **NVIDIA Groq 3 LPU**, Preferred Networks (PFN), Ambarella
- TSMC market share comparison: TSMC ~44% in 2026

## Capex (massive)

- **FY2026 capex revised up to ~110 trillion won (~$78B) — RECORD**
- Primary allocation:
  - **P4 plant in Pyeongtaek (Korea)** — memory capacity expansion
  - **Taylor wafer fab (US, Texas)** — foundry leading-edge

## 2027 supply outlook

- **Supply-demand gap to widen further in 2027** (Samsung's own warning)
- Validates structural shortage thesis across DRAM/HBM/NAND

## Strategic significance

- **Samsung "first to ship HBM4 at scale for Vera Rubin"** — conflicts with our prior framing of [[SK Hynix]] as dominant HBM4 supplier with ~70% NVIDIA share. Either Samsung qualified faster than expected, or the "at scale" framing is competitive marketing. Worth flagging on multiple pages.
- Samsung is **the only fully vertically integrated AI infrastructure player** — Memory + Foundry + LSI + Displays all under one roof
- Capex 110 trillion won = roughly 2× TSMC's $52-56B and 2× ASML's revenue base — single largest semiconductor capex commitment globally
