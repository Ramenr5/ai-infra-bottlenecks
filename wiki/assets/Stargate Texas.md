# Stargate Texas

The Texas portion of the [[Stargate]] portfolio — now confirmed via [[Stargate Project announcement (2025)]] to be **a multi-site Texas footprint anchored by [[Stargate Abilene]]**, not 20 generic sites in a single county. Texas hosts the bulk of Stargate's US capacity but is part of a broader 7-state/international footprint.

## Named Texas sites (per OpenAI Sept 2025 expansion announcement)

- **[[Stargate Abilene]]** — flagship; 10 datacenters; first JPMorgan $2.3B debt financing landed here (May 2025)
- **Shackelford County, TX**
- **Milam County, TX**

## Earlier framing (now superseded)

[[Situational Awareness, Two Years Later (2026)|Ansari's April 2026 piece]] described "20 datacenters in Texas, 500K sqft each, 10 GW total." The 20-sites/10GW framing applies to **Stargate as a whole, not just Texas** — the actual Texas-only count is 3 named sites + possible additional projects. The 10 GW figure is the cumulative Stargate target, not Texas-specific.

## Scale comparison

- **10 GW** ≈ total electricity consumption of New York City + San Diego combined (per [[Situational Awareness, Two Years Later (2026)]])
- ≈ 2–3× the total IT load of [[Northern Virginia]] (today's largest US datacenter cluster)
- Largest single-vehicle datacenter buildout in history

## Status

- Announced January 2025 as part of $500B Stargate commitment
- Construction underway as of April 2026 (per source)
- Cadence (serial / parallel / staged) not specified in source

## Why it matters

- **Power layer:** single biggest concrete demand-side data point in this wiki. If Stargate Texas delivers on schedule, ERCOT must accommodate 10 GW of new firm AI load on top of baseline state growth — a meaningful fraction of total ERCOT peak demand.
- **Geographic concentration:** doubles down on [[Texas]] as the dominant US AI infra geography (vs. [[Northern Virginia]]). ERCOT's permitting speed and gas-rich generation mix are likely the deciding factors.
- **Counterparty risk:** the whole asset depends on [[OpenAI]] as offtake anchor. If OpenAI's compute demand shifts (toward inference-time scaling vs. training, or toward different geographies), the asset's load factor changes.
