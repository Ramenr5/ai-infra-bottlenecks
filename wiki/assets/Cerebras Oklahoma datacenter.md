# Cerebras Oklahoma datacenter

[[Cerebras Systems]]'s flagship operating datacenter. Specifically called out in [[Cerebras — Faster Tokens Please (2026)]] for its facility-side cooling architecture — designed to handle the WSE-3's high power density and unusually cold inlet-temperature requirement.

## Key facts

- **Location:** Oklahoma City, OK
- **Operator:** Cerebras Systems
- **Status:** Operating
- **Chiller plant:** **6,000 tons** producing **5°C (42°F) chilled water**, warmed across a heat exchanger to ~21°C (70°F) before reaching the engine block
- *For reference:* GB200 NVL72 systems are specified up to 45°C inlet temperature — Cerebras requires a much colder envelope, making facilities "chiller-heavy"

## Why it matters

- **Concrete operating asset** for tracking Cerebras's 750 MW [[OpenAI]] capacity commitment through 2028 — Oklahoma is part of the base from which capacity additions are measured
- **Geographic precedent:** validates **[[Oklahoma]]** as a viable AI-infra location alongside [[Texas]], [[Northern Virginia]], and [[Ohio]]. Different grid (SPP), different climate, different permitting environment
- **Facility-side cooling design point:** the 6,000-ton chiller plant is meaningful scale and demonstrates that wafer-scale accelerators impose non-standard facility requirements. Argues for higher Trane/LiquidStack/Vertiv content per Cerebras MW vs. standard GB200 deployments
