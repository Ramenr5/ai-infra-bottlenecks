# Susquehanna nuclear plant

A **2.5 GW** nuclear power station in Pennsylvania, operated by [[Talen Energy]]. Subject of the landmark behind-the-meter contract to supply **960 MW** of dedicated capacity to an adjoining [[Amazon]] data center campus — the canonical case study for hyperscaler / existing-nuclear pairing.

## Key facts

- **Nameplate:** 2.5 GW (two reactors)
- **Operator:** [[Talen Energy]]
- **Grid:** PJM (mid-Atlantic)
- **Amazon contract:** 960 MW behind-the-meter, dedicated to adjoining data center campus
- **Regulatory status:** Initial contract challenged at FERC by neighboring utilities (AEP, Exelon) seeking to cap Amazon at lower MW level

## Why it matters

- **Existence proof** for the model of carving existing nuclear capacity out of the grid into hyperscaler dedicated supply
- **Speed-of-deployment advantage:** uses an asset that's already operating, sidestepping the 10+ year timeline for new nuclear construction ([[Power & energy]] notes recent >3 GW power station builds took ~5 years even excluding nuclear-specific permitting)
- **Pricing precedent:** establishes a comparable for how much hyperscalers pay for firm, low-carbon, behind-the-meter generation. Other Constellation / Vistra deals reference this anchor.
- **Regulatory test case:** the FERC fight determines whether this model scales or stays narrow

## Adjacent / similar potential assets

- Other existing US nuclear plants where capacity could be carved out: Constellation's Three Mile Island Unit 1 (Microsoft restart deal), Vistra's Comanche Peak, Duke / Southern Company fleets
- Most existing US nuclear plants are >2 GW and located near transmission backbones — physically pairable with data center development
