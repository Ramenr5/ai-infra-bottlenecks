# TSMC Advanced Backend Fab 6

[[TSMC]]'s newest advanced packaging facility in Taiwan, opened in 2023. The single largest near-term lever for relieving the [[CoWoS packaging capacity]] bottleneck for AI chips.

## Key facts

- **Location:** Taiwan
- **Operator:** [[TSMC]]
- **Opened:** 2023
- **Full capacity:** up to **83,000 wafers/month** of advanced packaging (per Epoch / TSMC PR)
- **Impact:** more than doubles TSMC's total advanced packaging volume when fully ramped

## Why it matters

- **CoWoS is the binding constraint** on AI accelerator output at the frontier (see [[CoWoS packaging capacity]]). Fab 6 is the primary supply-side answer through 2026.
- **Critical dependency for all NVIDIA frontier GPUs**, plus AMD MI300/MI350 and several hyperscaler ASICs going through TSMC packaging
- **Ramp pace governs how much GPU output industry-wide expands.** TSMC announced 60%/yr CoWoS capacity growth through 2026 — Fab 6 is the main vehicle for that

## Open questions

- Actual ramp pace vs. announced — has Fab 6 hit 83K wafers/mo as of the most recent quarter?
- Yield curves on advanced packaging at this scale (CoWoS-L vs CoWoS-S vs SoIC)
- Geopolitical concentration risk — entirely Taiwan-located; no comparable advanced-packaging asset elsewhere
- TSMC's next-generation packaging (SoIC, system-on-wafer) deployment plan — does Fab 6 convert, or do new fabs come online?
