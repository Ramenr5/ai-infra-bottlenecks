# Marvell Technology

**Custom AI ASIC design partner + optical DSP supplier.** Sits between [[Broadcom]] (custom ASIC + networking) and [[Astera Labs]] (connectivity silicon) on the picks-and-shovels map. Less concentrated on AI than Broadcom but broader optical interconnect business.

## Two distinct AI businesses

### Custom AI ASIC

- **Revenue: ~zero in FY26 → $1.5B annual run rate currently**
- **>20% YoY growth guided for FY27** (faster than previously guided)
- **Microsoft Maia** is the publicly known customer
- Other customers rumored but not disclosed; multi-billion-dollar relationships

### Optical / Interconnect

- **50%+ YoY growth guided for FY27** (faster than custom ASIC)
- Drivers: **800G + 1.6T optical DSPs** (the chips inside transceivers)
- CXL switch attach rates
- Eventual Photonic Fabric ramps

The optical DSPs go inside transceivers from [[Coherent]], [[Lumentum]], Fabrinet, and others — **doubly-exposed to scale-out networking**.

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **Hyperscaler custom ASIC duopoly with [[Broadcom]].** Broadcom = Google TPU + Meta MTIA; Marvell = Microsoft Maia + (rumored) others. Hyperscaler internalization of compute is a real and growing pipeline; both names benefit.
2. **Optical DSP exposure is structural.** Every 800G/1.6T transceiver in an AI data center has a Marvell or Broadcom DSP inside. Marvell's 50% optical growth guide for FY27 = direct read on scale-out networking buildout pace.
3. **50% optical growth > 20% custom ASIC growth** = interesting signal that connectivity is the more constrained segment of the AI infrastructure stack in 2026-27

## Counterparties / competitive frame

- [[Broadcom]] — direct comp; bigger AI ASIC business but Marvell catching up
- [[Coherent]], [[Lumentum]], Fabrinet — customers for Marvell optical DSPs
- [[NVIDIA]] = both partner (NVIDIA buys some Marvell silicon for networking) and competitor (NVIDIA increasingly designing its own networking silicon via Mellanox)
- Storage / networking legacy business is winding down; AI is the future

## Strategic position

- Less concentrated on Broadcom-style "$100B AI 2027" guide framing
- More diversified across ASIC + optical + storage/networking legacy
- Stock historically more volatile than AVGO (smaller, less diversified by end-market)
