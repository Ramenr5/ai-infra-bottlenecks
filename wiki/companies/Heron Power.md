# Heron Power

US-based Solid State Transformer (SST) startup focused on direct **medium-voltage-to-800VDC** units. Building a **40 GW US manufacturing facility** for its 4.2 MW Heron Link product — the largest disclosed SST manufacturing commitment.

## Key facts

- **Product**: Heron Link — 4.2 MW MV-input SST
- **Efficiency target**: 98.5% MV-to-rack
- **Manufacturing**: 40 GW US facility (under build, capacity disclosed)
- **Approach**: direct MV-input (vs DG Matrix/Amperesand's LV-input-first strategy) — collapses LV transformer + rectifier into a single device
- **Status**: pre-UL-certification as of May 2026

## Why it matters for the thesis

- **40 GW manufacturing facility = largest US SST capacity commitment** disclosed publicly
- Direct MV-input strategy = differentiated approach; if 3,300V+ SiC supply (Wolfspeed 10 kV March 2026) ramps fast enough, Heron's path is shorter to Phase 4 endgame than LV-first competitors
- Pure-play exposure to [[800VDC transition]] Phase 4 (SST end-state, >2029)

## Risks

- 3,300V+ SiC supply still in limited production; depends on [[Wolfspeed]] / Infineon ramp
- 40 GW US facility is a *capacity* figure, not a *commit-from-customers* figure — execution risk
- Pre-UL-cert; every deployment requires per-site AHJ approval until NEC 2029 partial provision lands
- Competition: [[DG Matrix]] has the NVIDIA MGX reference slot; [[Novos Power]] claims 50% smaller footprint
