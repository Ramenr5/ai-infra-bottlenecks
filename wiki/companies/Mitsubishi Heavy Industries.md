# Mitsubishi Heavy Industries

Japanese industrial conglomerate (TSE: 7011); via **Mitsubishi Power**, one of the big-three heavy-duty gas turbine OEMs alongside [[GE Vernova]] and [[Siemens Energy]]. A direct beneficiary of the gas-turbine supercycle driven by AI datacenter power demand.

## Why it matters for the thesis

- **Third of the gas-turbine oligopoly** — the 60/40 gas/renewable mix for US incremental generation ([[Goldman Sachs - Generational Growth (2024)]]) flows to GEV / Siemens Energy / MHI
- **Capacity expansion**: Mitsubishi Power targeting **~+30% production increase** (per [[SemiAnalysis - Onsite Gas Deep Dive (2025)]]) to meet datacenter-driven turbine demand
- **Onsite-gas play**: heavy-duty + aeroderivative turbines for behind-the-meter datacenter power (bypassing the grid interconnect queue)
- **J-class turbines**: among the highest-efficiency heavy-duty units, relevant for large combined-cycle datacenter campuses

## Why "watch the turbine OEMs"

Gas turbine backlogs are a [[Power & energy]] leading indicator. GEV (110 GW target) and Siemens Energy (record orders) are primary-confirmed; MHI completes the oligopoly. Combined, they gate how fast the gas-fired answer to the power bottleneck can scale.
