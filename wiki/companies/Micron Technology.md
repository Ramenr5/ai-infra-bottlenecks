# Micron Technology

**Third-source HBM supplier** after [[SK Hynix]] (43%) and Samsung (33%); Micron holds ~24% of 2026 HBM market. The **only US-headquartered memory supplier** at scale — natural beneficiary of CHIPS Act incentives and supply-chain diversification. Q2 FY26 numbers are extraordinary (revenue +196% YoY, 75% GM).

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **Third-source primary on [[HBM allocation]].** When all three suppliers are reporting record results with sold-out backlog, the bottleneck severity is unambiguous.
2. **HBM4 NVIDIA Vera Rubin alignment** = Micron is sole-sourced or majority-sourced for some Rubin-class SKUs (taking share from SK Hynix at the margin)
3. **75% GM → 81% guide** = HBM is the highest-margin segment in semiconductors right now; operating leverage extraordinary
4. **US-headquartered diversification angle** — political and supply-chain-resilience tailwind; CHIPS Act exposure
5. **HBM4E in development for 2027** = supply window past current ramp; aligns with [[TSMC]]'s 24-HBM-stack roadmap for 2029

## Counterparties / competitive frame

- [[SK Hynix]] — dominant HBM peer; Micron taking some share with HBM4
- Samsung Memory — comparable scale; HBM4 qualification with NVIDIA in progress
- [[NVIDIA]] — anchor customer for HBM; Vera Rubin uses Micron HBM4 for some SKUs
- [[AMD]], hyperscaler ASICs — also HBM customers, less concentrated
- Storage (NAND) is the other Micron segment; less AI-driven than DRAM/HBM

## Strategic position

- US headquartering = unique geopolitical positioning (only US HBM supplier at scale)
- Smaller than SK Hynix and Samsung in absolute capacity but growing share rapidly
- HBM4 NVIDIA Vera Rubin win is the single most important commercial event
- Margins comparable to SK Hynix (47-72%); peak-cycle economics extreme
