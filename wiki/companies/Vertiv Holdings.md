# Vertiv Holdings

US datacenter critical-infrastructure leader (NYSE: VRT). **#1 in the liquid-cooling market** per Dell'Oro. Broad portfolio: thermal management (air + liquid), power (UPS, busway, switchgear), and racks/enclosures — one of the purest large-cap plays on datacenter physical buildout.

## Key facts

- **Market position**: leading the datacenter liquid-cooling market ([[Dell'Oro - Liquid Cooling Market (2026)]])
- **Portfolio**: thermal (CDUs, cold plates, RDHx, chillers, CRAH), power (UPS, PDU, busway, switchgear), integrated racks, services
- **End markets**: hyperscale, colocation, enterprise, edge
- **Liquid-cooling TAM**: $3B (2025) → $7B (2029), >$15B over 5 yr — Vertiv is the share leader
- **800VDC adjacency**: also exposed to the power-distribution transition (no named 800VDC switchboard yet per [[SemiAnalysis - Inside the 800VDC Revolution (2026)]], but incumbent positioning)

## Why it matters for the thesis

- **Highest-liquidity large-cap pure-ish play** on datacenter physical capex (cooling + power), exposed to BOTH the [[Datacenter cooling]] and [[800VDC transition]] bottlenecks
- Diversified across thermal AND power = less binary than cooling-only or SST-only names
- Direct beneficiary of the GB200/Rubin DLC mandate (120 kW/rack → DLC-exclusive)

## Risks

- CDU market may be saturating as competitors pile in ([[Dell'Oro - Liquid Cooling Market (2026)]] flags this)
- Hyperscalers developing proprietary cooling (AWS built its own CDU) = insourcing risk
- Valuation has re-rated hard on the AI-cooling narrative — priced for continued share gains
- Air-cooling legacy revenue exposed if transition is faster than expected
