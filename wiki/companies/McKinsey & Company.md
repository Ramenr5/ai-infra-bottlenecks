# McKinsey & Company

Global management consultancy. **Tracked here as an analyst shop** — their TMT and Industrials practices have published the most-cited AI infrastructure capex forecasts in the buildout era.

## Why tracked as an analyst

McKinsey reports function as **primary TAM-anchoring sources** in the AI infrastructure conversation, alongside [[Goldman Sachs]] on power, [[Epoch AI]] on compute scaling, and [[SemiAnalysis]] on semiconductor specifics. Their figures get cited and re-cited across investor presentations, hyperscaler IR decks, and policy debates.

## Notable AI infrastructure research

- **[[McKinsey - The Cost of Compute (2025)|The cost of compute: A $7 trillion race to scale data centers]]** (April 2025) — the canonical primary source for $5.2T AI / $6.7T total / $7.9T accelerated DC capex through 2030
- **The data center dividend** — capacity / capex perspective for cloud providers
- **Beyond compute: Infrastructure that powers and cools AI data centers** — physical-layer focus
- **Scaling bigger, faster, cheaper data centers** — operations focus
- **The data center balance: How US states can navigate** — policy / regional focus

## Position vs. other analyst shops

| Shop | Strength |
| --- | --- |
| [[Goldman Sachs]] | Power-side, utility forecasts, generation mix |
| **McKinsey** | **Capex sizing, TAM, demand-side scenarios** |
| [[Epoch AI]] | Compute scaling, training-side feasibility |
| [[SemiAnalysis]] | Silicon supply chain, kernel-level analysis, tokenomics |

McKinsey's bias is **top-down demand modeling** — they start with token-generation forecasts and work backwards to required capacity and capex. This contrasts with Epoch's bottom-up bottleneck analysis and SemiAnalysis's chip-level supply analysis.

## Beyond research — also a deal advisor

- Strategic advisory on hyperscaler infrastructure decisions
- M&A and capital-raising mandates in the AI infra sector
- Not a direct investor

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

- **Sizes the upper bound** of the investable opportunity ($6.7T total / $5.2T AI through 2030)
- **Demand-side framework** is the complement to Epoch's supply-side framework. Combined they give a full picture: McKinsey says "the demand exists and will be funded"; Epoch says "the supply can technically be built"; the gap is the bottleneck thesis itself.
- **Caveats:** McKinsey numbers tend to be aggressive (top-down demand TAM). The IT Brew critique notes the $7T scenario isn't necessarily *fundable* given power/chip/capital constraints.

## What to watch

- Annual updates to the Cost of Compute thesis
- Regional / state-level breakdowns when published
- Their take on capex sustainability if a hyperscaler ever pulls back
