# Wolfspeed

US silicon carbide (SiC) semiconductor pure-play (NYSE: WOLF). **10 kV SiC MOSFET commercially available as bare die March 2026** — the breakthrough that opens direct medium-voltage rectifier and SST architectures in 800VDC datacenters.

## Key facts

- **10 kV SiC MOSFET**: commercially available as **bare die since March 2026**
- **What it enables**: direct rectification from MV (13.8-34.5 kV) — previously gated by absence of >10 kV commercial parts
- **End markets**: AI datacenters, EV traction, grid-tied power electronics, industrial drives
- **Competitive context**: SST vendors using 3,300V-class SiC (DG Matrix, Heron Power, Amperesand) → 10 kV opens a new architectural class

## Why it matters for the thesis

- **10 kV bare die = the single semiconductor enabler** of Phase 3 MV rectifier and Phase 4 SST end-state architectures in [[800VDC transition]]
- Pure-play SiC = highest beta exposure to the underlying power-electronics revolution (vs Infineon's broader semi portfolio)
- US-headquartered = US strategic semi exposure (vs Asia-focused competitors)

## Risks

- Operating losses historically; capital structure tight
- Competition from Infineon (broader SiC + GaN), STMicroelectronics, onsemi
- Yield ramp at 10 kV is still early — bare die ≠ mass-produced packaged modules
- High-cost-per-die at MV ratings; volume economics improve only as Phase 4 SST deployments scale (>2029)
