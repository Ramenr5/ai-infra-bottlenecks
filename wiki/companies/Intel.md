# Intel

**Integrated Device Manufacturer (IDM)** — designs and manufactures own chips, plus operates Intel Foundry for external customers. The only US-headquartered leading-edge logic manufacturer. After ~5 years of trailing TSMC, **18A is the first Intel node to lead** (in feature density terms). Q1 2026 marks 6 consecutive earnings beats — the "New Intel" turnaround narrative.

## 18A / Panther Lake — the inflection node

- **Panther Lake (Intel internal CPU)** launched January 2026 — first product on 18A
- **Volume ramping 6-7× QoQ** in Q2 2026
- Q2 gross margin 41% → 39% on Panther Lake ramp
- **Yields hitting year-end target by mid-year** per CFO Zinsner
- 18A is Intel's first leading-edge node after ~5 years trailing TSMC

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **TSMC alternative at the foundry level — real but small.** Intel Foundry at $5.4B/quarter vs TSMC's $35.9B/quarter, but the operating loss is narrowing and 18A is real product. Adds **optionality** to the foundry concentration story.
2. **Only US-headquartered IDM at leading edge.** CHIPS Act exposure + supply chain resilience play. Geopolitically important.
3. **Gaudi 4 = 6% accelerator share** = small but non-zero AI accelerator footprint (alongside [[AMD]]); provides some price tension on NVIDIA dominance.
4. **AI businesses = 60% of total revenue** — Intel has transformed by revenue mix. Comparable to AMD's transformation arc.
5. **18A is the inflection node** — if Intel Foundry wins meaningful external customers on 18A/18AP, [[Lithography monopoly]] and [[CoWoS packaging capacity]] dynamics shift; if 18A flops, foundry ambitions stall and [[TSMC]] concentration deepens.

## Standard cell library / EDA caveat (from [[The EDA Primer From RTL to Silicon (2026)]])

Intel's initial 18A standard cell library had **only 4 Vt options** (Intel used 3) vs **6 at TSMC** — limiting designers' Pareto-optimal flexibility. **18AP fixes this**, expanding Vt options. This was a real technical gap that affected early customer adoption; 18AP closes it.

## Strategic position

- IDM model = designs AND manufactures, vs fabless (NVIDIA, AMD) or pure-foundry (TSMC)
- Foundry side requires winning external customers — partial decoupling from internal product success
- **CEO Lip-Bu Tan** (appointed early 2025) driving turnaround; ex-Cadence
- Rumored potential Foundry spinoff as separate entity — would clarify business model
- **Apple precedent** — Apple absorbed ~90% of TSMC 3nm in 2023; Intel needs anchor 18A external customer of comparable significance

## Counterparties / competitive frame

- [[TSMC]] — primary competitor at foundry; Intel currently ~1/7 the size; TSMC ~44% market share 2026
- [[Samsung Electronics]] Foundry — third foundry contender; SF2 yields 55-60% early 2026; AI customer wins include NVIDIA Groq 3 LPU, Tesla $16.5B; both Intel and Samsung positioning as TSMC alternatives
- [[NVIDIA]] / [[AMD]] = primary competitors at AI accelerator; **AMD 5th gen EPYC actively taking share from Intel Xeon** (per [[AMD Q1 2026 earnings]]); Intel Gaudi 4 ~6% accelerator share
- [[ASML]] — Intel uses EUV/High-NA EUV for 18A
- [[Applied Materials]], [[Lam Research]], [[KLA Corporation]] — WFE suppliers
- US government — CHIPS Act funding receipts material to capex profile
