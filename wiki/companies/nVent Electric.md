# nVent Electric

Electrical-connection and protection company (NYSE: NVT). Strong share in datacenter liquid cooling — named by [[SemiAnalysis - Datacenter Anatomy Cooling Systems (2026)|SemiAnalysis]] as a rear-door heat exchanger (RDHx) maker and by [[Dell'Oro - Liquid Cooling Market (2026)|Dell'Oro]] as a liquid-cooling share leader.

## Key facts

- **Products**: RDHx, liquid-cooling distribution, enclosures, electrical connections/fastening, thermal management
- **Market position**: strong liquid-cooling share (Dell'Oro)
- **RDHx role**: rear-door heat exchangers bridge air→liquid for 30-50 kW racks (transition-era "bridge" solution)
- **End markets**: datacenter, industrial, infrastructure, energy

## Why it matters for the thesis

- Pure-ish exposure to the [[Datacenter cooling]] transition via RDHx + liquid distribution
- RDHx is the "bridge" technology SemiAnalysis says will proliferate because there aren't enough DLC-ready datacenters — nVent benefits from the messy middle of the transition
- Smaller / more focused than [[Vertiv Holdings]] = higher cooling-mix beta

## Risks

- RDHx is a transitional technology — if DLC-native shells ramp fast, the bridge market compresses
- Less brand dominance than Vertiv in CDUs
- Diversified electrical revenue dilutes datacenter-cooling pure-play exposure
