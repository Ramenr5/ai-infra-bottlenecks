# ASML

**Sole supplier of EUV lithography systems globally.** The most concentrated single-source supplier in the AI silicon supply chain — more concentrated than [[TSMC]] (which has Samsung Foundry + Intel Foundry as imperfect alternatives) or [[SK Hynix]] (which has Samsung + Micron). **No EUV alternative exists at any price.**

## Position in the supply chain

- **EUV (extreme ultraviolet) lithography:** sole supplier globally. Patterns sub-7nm features for TSMC N5/N3/N2, Samsung Foundry, Intel 18A and beyond.
- **High-NA EUV:** next-generation 0.55-NA systems enabling sub-2nm nodes. **2 shipped in Q1 2026** = material ramp signal.
- **DUV (deep ultraviolet):** mature/trailing node lithography; competes with Nikon, Canon at lower nodes but dominant overall.
- **Customer concentration:** TSMC is largest single customer; Samsung Foundry + Intel + Memory makers (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron) round out the top.

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

- **The most concentrated chokepoint in our entire wiki.** Even [[CoWoS packaging capacity]] has TSMC as a single supplier with planned 60%/yr capacity growth; ASML is a single supplier with much smaller tool-throughput growth (a single High-NA EUV machine costs ~$380M and takes years to install).
- **€38.8B backlog** = the buildout is **already booked through 2028+**. Forward visibility makes ASML one of the most predictable revenue streams in the industry.
- **HBM as second cycle leg** (per 2030 guide) means memory-side capex doesn't just fund [[SK Hynix]] / Samsung / Micron — it flows back to ASML for the lithography tools that make the HBM dies
- **Export controls** are the key risk — historically meaningful China revenue has been restricted; further tightening could pull material order book

## Real-world constraints on ramping

- A single EUV machine: ~$200M (low-NA) to ~$380M (High-NA)
- Installation: months to a year per machine
- Tool throughput limits the rate at which TSMC/etc. can scale wafer output
- ASML's own production capacity: ~50-60 EUV systems/year currently, scaling to ~70+

## Counterparties / competitive frame

- **No EUV competitor.** Nikon and Canon make DUV systems but never developed EUV.
- Adjacencies via WFE oligopoly: [[Applied Materials]] (deposition/etch/CMP), Lam Research (deposition/etch), KLA (metrology/inspection), Tokyo Electron (deposition/etch)
- ASML's monopoly position is structural — built on decades of R&D + ASML-Carl Zeiss optics JV that has no peer
