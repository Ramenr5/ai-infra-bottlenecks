**WFE oligopoly #2-3.** Global leader in **memory etch and deposition equipment** — particularly strong in DRAM and HBM tool segments. Co-coverage with [[Applied Materials]] (broader WFE) and [[KLA Corporation]] (process control).

## Memory segment (the HBM equipment story)

- **Memory revenue: 39% of systems revenue**
- **DRAM: 27% of systems revenue (RECORD)**
- **Global leader in memory etch + deposition** — direct beneficiary of [[HBM allocation]] supply expansion
- HBM ramp = direct driver of segment growth

## Strategic context

- Etch and deposition intensity dramatically increasing with new memory cycle (1C node transition)
- 1C transition **expands Lam's served available market by >20%**
- AI power/efficiency requirements driving advanced-generation device transitions
- Less direct foundry exposure than AMAT (memory-tilted)

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **Memory etch leadership = direct HBM exposure.** As [[SK Hynix]] expands M15X for HBM4, [[Samsung]] qualifies HBM4 with NVIDIA, [[Micron Technology]] ramps HBM3E/HBM4 — all three buy Lam equipment.
2. **Raised WFE forecast to $140B** = leading indicator on industry capex absorption; corroborates [[Applied Materials]]' "$1T industry 2026" CEO call
3. **Q4 FY26 guide sequential acceleration** = ongoing memory-side capex deployment
4. **Picks-and-shovels exposure to every memory fab buildout**
