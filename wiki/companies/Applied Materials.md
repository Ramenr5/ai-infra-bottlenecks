# Applied Materials

**Tier-1 Wafer Fab Equipment (WFE) supplier** — the largest WFE company by revenue. Sells the deposition, etch, CMP (chemical-mechanical planarization), and inspection tools that go into every semiconductor fab. Picks-and-shovels exposure to the entire fab buildout across foundries (TSMC, Samsung, Intel) and memory makers (SK Hynix, Samsung Memory, Micron).

- **AMAT order book is a 6-12 month leading indicator** of foundry capex actually deploying — useful for thesis-validation timing on [[Capex trajectory thesis]]

## WFE oligopoly position

AMAT is the largest of four major WFE vendors:

| Vendor | Focus | Relative size |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Applied Materials (AMAT)** | Deposition, etch, CMP, inspection | #1 by revenue |
| **Lam Research (LRCX)** | Deposition, etch (particularly memory) | #2-3 |
| **KLA Corporation (KLAC)** | Metrology / inspection (dominant) | #3-4 |
| **Tokyo Electron (8035.T)** | Deposition, etch, coater/developer | #2-3 (Japan) |

ASML is in a separate category (lithography monopoly).

## Counterparties / competitive frame

- Customers: every foundry + memory maker globally — concentrated but with significant Tier 2/3 demand
- China revenue under US export controls = the key risk
- Adjacent tier-2 WFE: Onto Innovation, Veeco, etc.
