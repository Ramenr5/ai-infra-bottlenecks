# SK Hynix

Dominant supplier of **High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM)** — the memory technology that gates frontier AI accelerator performance. NVIDIA's primary HBM source. The single most-mentioned name in the [[HBM allocation]] bottleneck conversation.

### HBM4 milestones

- Mass production began **September 2025**
- Shipments begin **Q4 2025**
- Full-scale sales expansion **2026**
- Per industry coverage: **NVIDIA HBM4 allocation ~70% to SK Hynix** (Samsung targeting earlier delivery for the remainder)

### Subsequent-quarter indicators (Q4 2025 / Q1 2026 coverage)

- HBM supply expected to **remain tight into 2027**
- KED Global cites **72% operating margin** for an early-2026 quarter (pending official confirmation)
- M15X facility expansion is the primary capex vehicle for 2026; specific dollar amounts not disclosed

### Competitive shift — Samsung HBM4 challenge

Per [[Samsung Q1 2026 earnings]], **Samsung claims first to ship HBM4 at scale for NVIDIA Vera Rubin**. This challenges the prior framing that SK Hynix held ~70% NVIDIA HBM4 allocation. Either:

1. Samsung qualified faster than expected and is taking real HBM4 share
2. The "first at scale" is competitive marketing positioning
3. Multiple suppliers are shipping HBM4 in parallel for different SKUs

Worth tracking quarterly — SK Hynix's NVIDIA primary status is now contested rather than uncontested. See [[HBM allocation]] for full discussion.

## Key numbers (per Epoch baseline, May 2024)

- **HBM sold out through 2026** (now verified by SK Hynix Q3 2025 release)
- Projected **~60% annual HBM demand growth** (revenue) medium-to-long term
- HBM as share of total DRAM market expected to reach **~20% in 2024** (vs. low single digits in 2022)
- One analyst estimate: **45% annual HBM production volume growth** 2023–2028

## Position in the supply chain

- **HBM technology leadership** — first to HBM3, leading on HBM3E and HBM4
- **NVIDIA primary** — sole-sourced or majority-sourced for current H100/H200/Blackwell HBM
- **Samsung qualification issues (2024 reporting)** have left SK Hynix with even more concentrated NVIDIA exposure
- HBM is part of broader DRAM business; capacity additions often come from *reallocating DRAM lines* to HBM (with second-order DRAM market pricing effects)

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

- **Rent capture on scarcity.** HBM gross margins meaningfully higher than commodity DRAM. SK Hynix earnings reflect this.
- **Capacity addition is multi-year.** New DRAM fabs (which HBM uses) have 2–3 year construction + ramp. The 2024–2026 supply window is locked.
- **Cross-effect on DRAM:** HBM cannibalization tightens regular DRAM supply, indirectly affecting PC/mobile/server memory pricing.
- **Geographic concentration:** Korean production (Hynix + Samsung) and US ([[Micron]]) — no Chinese frontier HBM yet, which makes export controls less effective at constraining China's AI training than chip controls.

## Counterparties
