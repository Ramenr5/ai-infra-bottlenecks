# Tokyo Electron

**Japanese WFE oligopoly member.** Dominant in **wafer probers (38%)** and meaningful in **etch (23%)** and **deposition (27%)**. The Japanese counterpoint to US WFE peers — different geopolitical/currency profile. Completes our WFE coverage alongside [[ASML]], [[Applied Materials]], [[Lam Research]], and [[KLA Corporation]].

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **Completes WFE coverage** — primary data now on all 5 oligopoly members
2. **TEL +20% WFE growth call for 2026/2027** corroborates [[Lam Research]]'s $140B WFE forecast and [[Applied Materials]]'s "$1T industry 2026" call from CEO Dickerson — three independent equipment vendors all pointing at the same supercycle
3. **Japanese geographic exposure** — different sensitivity to USD/yen, US-China export control regime (Japan has aligned with US controls but enforcement different)
4. **Wafer prober + coater/developer dominance** = unique segments not addressed by US peers; near-monopoly in coater/developer
5. **Less direct memory cycle exposure than [[Lam Research]]** — more diversified across foundry + memory

## Counterparties / competitive frame

- US WFE peers: AMAT, LRCX, KLA — each with regional/segment specializations
- Customers: every foundry + memory maker globally — diversified
- China revenue: Japanese export controls aligned with US controls but historically meaningful
- Mid-tier WFE: Screen Holdings (Japanese coater/developer competitor), Hitachi High-Tech
