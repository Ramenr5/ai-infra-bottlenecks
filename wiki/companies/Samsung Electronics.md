# Samsung Electronics

**The only fully vertically integrated AI infrastructure player.** Combines Memory (DRAM, HBM, NAND), Foundry (SF2/SF3/SF1.4), LSI (Exynos), and Displays in one conglomerate. Q1 2026 operating profit +756% YoY — a single quarter beat full-year 2025. **Now the largest semiconductor capex committer globally at ~110 trillion won (~$78B FY2026).**

Listed on Korean Stock Exchange (ticker: 005930.KS). US investor access primarily via ADRs and Korean ETFs.

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **Vertical integration moat.** Memory + Foundry + LSI under one roof = unique strategic position. When all segments are running at scale (as Q1 2026), operating leverage is extraordinary.
2. **HBM4 supplier dynamics in flux.** Samsung's claim of being "first to ship HBM4 at scale for Vera Rubin" — if accurate — materially shifts the [[HBM allocation]] supplier hierarchy. Either Samsung is taking real HBM4 share from [[SK Hynix]], or it's competitive positioning ahead of full ramp.
3. **NAND #1 supplier** with +55-60% QoQ pricing power validates [[NAND flash supply]] severity.
4. **110 trillion won capex** = largest single semiconductor capex commitment globally. Anchors [[Capex trajectory thesis]] supply-side at unprecedented scale.
5. **Samsung Foundry SF2 at 55-60% yield** = credible competitive option alongside [[Intel]] 18A. Three-foundry world potentially emerging behind [[TSMC]].
6. **Vera Rubin HBM4 base-die supply via Samsung's own Foundry** = unique vertical-integration play; Foundry Q2 driver explicitly cited as HBM4 base-die

## Counterparties / competitive frame

### Memory
- [[SK Hynix]] — direct HBM peer; was dominant; Samsung now contesting HBM4 share
- [[Micron Technology]] — third HBM supplier; smaller but also taking share
- [[SanDisk]], Kioxia, YMTC — NAND competitors

### Foundry
- [[TSMC]] — dominant; Samsung Foundry distant #2; ~44% vs Samsung's much smaller share
- [[Intel]] — third foundry contender; both Samsung and Intel positioning as TSMC alternatives

### IP / EDA
- [[ARM Holdings]] — Exynos uses Arm cores
- [[Synopsys]] / [[Cadence Design Systems]] — EDA tools for design

## Strategic position

- **Conglomerate exposure** = less pure-play than [[Micron Technology]] (memory-only) or [[Cadence Design Systems]] (EDA-only)
- **Korean listing** = limits US investor access; ADR + ETF exposure available
- **Politically important** to Korea — bilateral relations with US/China affect strategic positioning
- **CHIPS Act exposure** via Taylor TX fab construction
