# ABB

Swiss-Swedish power and automation incumbent (SIX: ABBN). **NVIDIA partner** (October 2025 announcement for modular power blocks). Owner of strategic stakes in SST landscape (backed [[DG Matrix]]). Deployed at scale in early 800VDC operator sites.

## Key facts

- **NVIDIA partnership** (Oct 2025): modular power block including distribution
- **DG Matrix backing**: strategic backer of [[DG Matrix]] SST startup
- **MV-UPS product**: HiPerGuard — 98% efficiency; **deployed at Applied Digital's 400 MW North Dakota AI campus**
- **Solid-state breakers**:
  - Emax 2 (1500V DC) — for solar, energy storage, marine
  - SACE Infinitus (solid-state, 1000V/2500A) — datacenter adaptation announced October 2025 with NVIDIA
- **DC busway**: publicly disclosed 800VDC busway program

## Why it matters for the thesis

- **NVIDIA partnership** + DG Matrix strategic backing = best-positioned diversified incumbent on the 800VDC transition
- Multi-product exposure: MV-UPS (HiPerGuard), solid-state breakers (SACE Infinitus), DC busway, SST (via DG Matrix stake) — every layer of the 800VDC stack
- Already-deployed MV-UPS at Applied Digital validates the architecture beyond hyperscaler PoCs

## Risks

- Listed in Switzerland — currency / accessibility friction for US investors (ADR is ABBNY)
- Diversified revenue means AI datacenter exposure dilutes within broader robotics/electrification segments
- Strategic stake in DG Matrix is upside if DG Matrix wins NVIDIA MGX share, but bounded
