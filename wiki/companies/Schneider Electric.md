# Schneider Electric

French power-management and digital-automation incumbent (Euronext Paris: SU). Major AC datacenter electrical supplier (UPS, switchboards, PDUs). **No named discrete 800VDC product as of May 2026** — slower 800VDC positioning than [[ABB]], [[Eaton]], [[Delta Electronics]].

## Key facts

- **Product range**: UPS, switchboards, switchgear, PDUs, busway — full AC datacenter electrical stack
- **800VDC positioning**: no named 800VDC switchboard or SST product per SemiAnalysis as of May 2026
- **Strengths**: large installed base, deep colocation operator relationships
- **Customer set**: hyperscale, colocation, commercial, industrial

## Why it matters for the thesis

- **Laggard signal**: if AC-distribution incumbents like Schneider don't accelerate 800VDC products by 2027, share moves to [[ABB]] / [[Eaton]] / pure-plays
- Watchlist candidate — any 800VDC product announcement is a positive catalyst given current absence
- Colocation operator relationships matter most in Phase 1-2 where the retrofit can be incremental

## Risks

- Behind on 800VDC vs [[ABB]] (NVIDIA partner) and [[Eaton]] (Resilient acquisition)
- AC-distribution revenue cannibalization risk as 800VDC takes share through 2030
- Reactive (rather than leading) positioning in standards bodies
