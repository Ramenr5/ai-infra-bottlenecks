# Broadcom

**The other dominant supplier of AI silicon after NVIDIA.** Designs custom AI accelerators (XPUs) for hyperscalers and produces near-monopoly Ethernet networking switch ASICs (Tomahawk, Jericho). Plus VMware (post-2023 acquisition) for infrastructure software. CEO Hock Tan committed to **>$100B AI chip revenue by 2027** — one of the largest forward AI commitments after NVIDIA's $1T Blackwell+Rubin guide.

## Custom AI ASICs (XPUs)

Broadcom designs custom AI accelerator chips for:

| Customer | Project | Status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Google** | TPU | Multi-year, multi-generation; flagship relationship |
| **Meta** | MTIA | Multi-billion-dollar relationship |
| **OpenAI** | (newer, recently disclosed) | Engaged design partner |
| **Anthropic** | (newer, recently disclosed) | Engaged design partner |

Broadcom is the **design partner / packaging coordinator**; TSMC manufactures the actual silicon.

## Networking ASIC dominance

- **Tomahawk 6** — industry's first **102.4 Tbps Ethernet switch ASIC**; entered volume March 2026
- **Jericho 4** — 51.2 Tbps fabric chip; shipping since Aug 2025
- Designed to interconnect **>1 million XPUs across data centers**
- Effectively monopoly in Ethernet-based data center fabric

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **$100B+ AI revenue line of sight by 2027** = largest forward AI commitment after NVIDIA. Anchors [[Capex trajectory thesis]] from a second tier-1 supplier's perspective.
2. **Custom ASIC partnership model is durable** — Google, Meta, OpenAI, Anthropic all designing custom silicon via Broadcom. Hyperscaler ASIC pipeline is real and growing.
3. **Tomahawk + Jericho near-monopoly in Ethernet networking ASICs** = picks-and-shovels on every AI data center switch. Direct exposure to scale-out networking.
4. **VMware integration** is separate from but accretive to AI thesis — provides infrastructure software cross-sell into AI deployments.
5. **Comparable concentration to TSMC at logic foundry layer** — Broadcom is the dominant non-NVIDIA design partner; few alternatives at scale.

## Counterparties / competitive frame

- [[NVIDIA]] — competes for hyperscaler compute share; cooperates on networking (some NVIDIA systems use Broadcom switches)
- [[Marvell Technology]] — direct comp on custom ASIC + optical DSP; Marvell smaller but growing
- Arista Networks — buyer of Broadcom networking ASICs for white-box switches
- All hyperscalers — both customers (custom ASIC) and increasingly competitors (in-house networking ASIC attempts)

## Strategic position

- $100B-revenue conglomerate (semis + software + IP) with AI as the dominant growth segment
- Hock Tan's M&A track record (VMware, Symantec, CA, etc.) = capital-allocation reputation
- Concentrated AI revenue exposure to 4 hyperscaler partnerships = customer concentration risk
