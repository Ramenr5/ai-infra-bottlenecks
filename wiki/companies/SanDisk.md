# SanDisk

**Pure-play NAND flash memory company** spun out of Western Digital in February 2025. The cleanest tradable exposure to the emerging **AI NAND flash shortage** thesis — distinct from [[Micron Technology]]'s mixed DRAM/HBM-dominant + NAND business.

| Drivers | AI-fueled enterprise storage + NAND price increases |

## AI / data center commentary

- **AI servers >45% of shipments**
- **Data center revenue: +64% QoQ to $440M**
- Hyperscaler cloud spending = primary structural driver

## NAND market thesis (new bottleneck — see [[NAND flash supply]])

- AI infrastructure buildout has created **structural NAND flash shortage expected to persist until 2028**
- NAND is distinct from HBM/DRAM — used for storage, model checkpoints, training data, KV cache offload
- Pricing power and gross margin expansion through 2026-2028

## Stock + sell-side view

- **Shares: <$50 (Feb 2025 spin from Western Digital) → ~$698 (April 2026) = +1,350% in 12 months**
- BofA (Wamsi Mohan): **$1,080 price target** (up from $900), Buy
- Evercore ISI (Amit Daryanani): **$1,200 PT**, Outperform
- Bull thesis: "AI inference makes NAND more indispensable"; "more durable NAND cycle supported by AI-led demand and tighter supply"
- Stock has rallied hard but sell-side sees 50%+ further upside

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **Surfaces NAND as a previously-uncovered bottleneck.** New [[NAND flash supply]] page.
2. **Pure-play NAND exposure** = cleanest single name for the NAND-shortage trade vs [[Micron Technology]] (which has DRAM/HBM dominance)
3. **64% QoQ DC revenue growth** = explosive AI storage demand
4. **2028 supply tightness duration** comparable to HBM bottleneck timeline
5. **+1,350% stock move** = market already pricing the thesis, but room remains per sell-side

## Strategic position

- Less HBM exposure = different cycle dynamics than Micron/Hynix
