# Quanta Services

**Largest US power-delivery / electric-EPC contractor.** Installs the transmission, substations, distribution, and increasingly transformer manufacturing for utilities + IPPs + hyperscalers. **Record $48.5B backlog ($40.1B Electric segment)** = multi-year forward visibility on data center power infrastructure work. Investing $500-700M to **double transformer manufacturing capacity**.

## Why it matters

1. **Picks-and-shovels exposure to every hyperscaler data center power build-out** — agnostic to which hyperscaler wins; PWR installs the wires regardless
2. **$40.1B Electric backlog** = multi-year forward visibility on power-EPC work; comparable to [[ASML]] backlog dynamics for foundry capacity
3. **Doubling transformer manufacturing capacity** directly addresses the transformer-lead-time bottleneck flagged in [[Power & energy]]
4. **Beneficiary of every named project** — [[Stargate]] / [[Susquehanna nuclear plant]] / [[Cerebras Oklahoma datacenter]] all need PWR-installed power infrastructure
5. Different from utility plays (NextEra, Vistra) — Quanta sells labor + materials per project, not power

## Strategic position

- Largest electric EPC contractor in US
- Acquired Cupertino Electric (2023) + multiple other rollups = scale economics
- Vertical integration into transformer manufacturing = capturing more of the value chain
- Less exposed to utility regulatory cycles than the utilities themselves
- Direct beneficiary of hyperscaler capex acceleration

## Counterparties / supply chain

- Customers: utilities (PJM, ERCOT, etc.) + hyperscalers + IPPs ([[Talen Energy]], NextEra, Vistra)
- Equipment suppliers: [[GE Vernova]] (gas turbines), [[Siemens Energy]] (grid/transformers), Eaton, Hubbell
- Labor: IBEW journeymen, lineworkers

## What to watch

- Backlog growth quarterly (currently $48.5B record)
- Transformer manufacturing ramp milestones
- Margin trajectory on data center-specific projects
- Labor cost inflation (electrician/lineworker shortage)
- Specific project wins disclosure (Stargate, hyperscaler campuses)
