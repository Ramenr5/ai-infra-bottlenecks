## Gas Services segment (the data center engine)

- **Orders +32.4% to €8.87B** — largest business contributor
- Revenue +15% to €3.48B
- **"US demand largely driven by data centers"** — explicit attribution from earnings call
- **US orders nearly doubled to €6.94B**
- US revenue +45.7% to €2.75B

## Strategic position

- European counterpart to [[GE Vernova]] in gas turbines
- Also has grid services + transformers + wind (similar segment mix to GEV)
- Beneficiary of Goldman 60/40 gas/renewable mix in new US generation
- Less direct US presence than GEV but rapidly expanding (US orders nearly doubled)

## Counterparties / competitive frame

- [[GE Vernova]] = primary global competitor
- [[Mitsubishi Heavy Industries]] = third major gas turbine OEM
- Customers: utilities + IPPs globally + hyperscalers
- [[Quanta Services]] = installation partner for SE equipment in US

## Why it matters

1. **Independent EU confirmation** of US gas turbine demand surge (parallel to [[GE Vernova]] Q1)
2. **Backlog €154B** = multi-year visibility growing
3. **FY26 FCF guide raised from €4-5B to €8B** = profitability inflection from data center mix
4. **US orders nearly doubled** = Siemens Energy displacing some GE Vernova share, OR US market large enough for both to grow
5. Cross-check: two independent gas turbine OEMs confirming same demand wave

## Why "ENR.DE" not ADR

European-listed; ADR (SMNEY/SMNSY) exists but has lower liquidity. Primary trading is on Frankfurt Xetra.
