# Talen Energy

Independent power producer (IPP) operating the [[Susquehanna nuclear plant]] in Pennsylvania. Counterparty to Amazon's landmark 960 MW behind-the-meter nuclear deal — the canonical case study for hyperscaler / nuclear-asset pairing.

## Key deal

- **960 MW PPA / asset sale** to [[Amazon]] for the data center campus adjoining the [[Susquehanna nuclear plant]] (2.5 GW total nameplate)
- Structured as on-site behind-the-meter rather than grid-purchased — avoids transmission charges and guarantees firm supply
- **Deal challenged** by neighboring utilities (AEP, Exelon) at FERC, arguing it shifts grid costs to other ratepayers — material precedent for how regulators may treat future hyperscaler-IPP deals
- Per [[Can AI scaling continue through 2030 (2024)]]: most-cited deal of its kind, even as Amazon's bid was capped at lower MW initially

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

- **Existence proof for nuclear-DC pairing.** Validates that existing nuclear assets can be carved out for hyperscaler offtake — a faster path than new nuclear construction.
- **Regulatory precedent.** The FERC fight is the canonical case for whether behind-the-meter mega-PPAs are allowed to bypass shared grid costs. The outcome shapes whether this model scales or stays bespoke.
- **Asset re-rating dynamic.** Existing nuclear (and dispatchable gas) plants become more valuable as AI demand discovers them. Talen's market cap reflects this shift.

## Other US IPPs in similar position

- [[Vistra]] — Texas/PJM merchant fleet, including nuclear (Comanche Peak)
- [[Constellation Energy]] — largest US nuclear operator, multiple hyperscaler conversations
- [[NextEra Energy]] — diversified renewables + some nuclear

## Open questions

- FERC ruling outcome on the AEP/Exelon challenge — does the 960 MW deal stand, get reduced, or get reshaped?
- Talen's plans for additional capacity or other behind-the-meter deals
- How many other existing US nuclear plants could be carved out for hyperscalers (Constellation has signaled multiple in negotiation)?
