# Aran Industries

US AI-native EPC startup. Delivers **PE-stampable 800VDC engineering packages** bridging power electronics design, grid-level dynamic modeling, and regulatory engagement — capabilities incumbent EPCs don't combine.

## Key facts

- **Service**: PE-stampable 800VDC engineering packages (Professional Engineer-stamped, ready for AHJ approval)
- **Positioning**: AI-native EPC — built specifically for the 800VDC + GW-scale interconnection challenge
- **Contributed to**: [[SemiAnalysis - Inside the 800VDC Revolution (2026)]] (acknowledged contributor)
- **Capability set**: power electronics design + grid dynamic modeling + interconnection regulatory engagement (NERC, ERCOT)

## Why it matters for the thesis

- **800VDC moves grid-facing behavior into software-defined power electronics** (SST control algorithms, converter current limits, DC bus capacitance) → traditional EPCs lack the power-electronics + grid-dynamics combined capability
- **NERC Level 3 Essential Actions Alert (May 2026)** + ERCOT NOGRR282 raised the regulatory bar — Aran's capability set is exactly what these new rules require
- The "Computational Load Entity" registration framework (1 MW+ within 20 MW+ aggregate at 60 kV+) drives demand for PE-stamped engineering packages
- Pre-2029 pre-NEC, every 800VDC site requires custom AHJ approval — Aran's PE stamps unlock that

## Risks

- Private company; access depends on whether it goes public or remains private
- Scale-up risk: PE-engineering bandwidth scales with people, not with software multipliers
- Incumbent EPC pushback (Bechtel, Fluor, Black & Veatch) could integrate power-electronics capability over time
