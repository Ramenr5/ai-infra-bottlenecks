## The OpenAI $300B agreement

- **$300B contract value** with [[OpenAI]] for data center capacity
- **4.5 GW of planned facilities**
- Part of $500B [[Stargate]] initiative (Oracle + SoftBank + NVIDIA + OpenAI)
- Primary driver of Oracle's RPO surge to $553B

## Strategic position

- **"GPU landlord" business model** — Oracle owns/operates the data center; OpenAI consumes the compute
- Different from MSFT/GOOGL/AMZN traditional hyperscaler model (sells compute to many customers)
- More like project finance: 1 anchor customer, 1 long-term contract, single-purpose data centers
- **High concentration risk** — OpenAI relationship is existential
- 30,000 layoffs = capital reallocation toward infra (away from legacy software ops)

### ByteDance + Johor Malaysia (the under-appreciated angle)

Per [[SemiAnalysis - Oracle Winning AI Compute (2025)]] (June 2025):
- **ByteDance was Oracle's largest GPU customer** (pre-$300B OpenAI scale-up)
- ByteDance "comparable in scale to US hyperscalers" as GPU consumer
- **APAC cluster: 600-700 MW within a year; potentially 2 GW by 2028**
- Concentrated in **[[Johor Malaysia]]** = "world's second-largest AI hub"

### Operational scale

- **Oracle leased >2 GW of DC capacity Nov 2023 → Jan 2025** — single largest US DC lessor in that period
- Abilene progression: 220 MW → 660 MW → ~880 MW (OpenAI deal phasing)
- Larry Ellison (June 2025): >$130B contracts in next 12 months — aged perfectly to $553B RPO by Q3 FY26
- **Gold ClusterMAX rating** on RoCEv2 networking

## Counterparties

- [[OpenAI]] = anchor customer ($300B / 4.5 GW)
- [[Stargate]] = JV vehicle (Oracle is one of 4 equity partners)
- [[NVIDIA]] = primary GPU supplier
- [[Stargate Abilene]] = key asset; Oracle operates compute, JPMorgan-financed
- Banks for $45-50B raise: not disclosed publicly

## Why it matters

1. **$553B RPO** = larger than MSFT Q3 revenue + AMZN Q1 revenue combined; but extremely concentrated on OpenAI
2. **GPU landlord model** = new business archetype; if successful, template for [[Stargate]]-style mega-deals
3. **FY26 capex $50B (up from $21B)** = Oracle now hyperscaler-tier committer despite very different business profile
4. **$45-50B debt + equity raise** = concrete primary data on [[AI debt financing capacity]] absorbing real volumes
5. **Concentration risk** = if OpenAI relationship sours, Oracle has $300B of committed capex with no obvious alternative
