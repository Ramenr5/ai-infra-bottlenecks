# Infineon

German power semiconductor incumbent (XETRA: IFX, ADR: IFNNY). Broad portfolio across **SiC, 650V GaN, BBU power modules**, and Partial Power Converter technology for 800VDC datacenter applications.

## Key facts

- **SiC supply to [[DG Matrix]]** — strategic supply relationship enabling DG Matrix's Interport SST
- **650V GaN FETs** — the workhorse semiconductor for Diablo 400 ±400V bipolar architectures (each rail at 400V from ground)
- **BBU roadmap** (announced March 2025): modular 4 kW Partial Power Converter cards parallel to **12 kW per unit at up to 99.5% peak efficiency**
- **SST size-reduction claim**: 40× weight, 14× size reduction vs conventional iron-core transformer (from Infineon's marketing on SST applications)
- **End markets**: AI datacenters, EV, industrial drives, renewables

## Why it matters for the thesis

- **Most-diversified 800VDC semiconductor enabler** — SiC + GaN + BBU power modules + SST-enabling tech all in one company
- 650V GaN supply for the EV-leveraged Diablo 400 ±400V bipolar architecture (Google: "selecting 400 VDC allows us to leverage the supply chain established by electric vehicles")
- BBU module scale-up (5.5 kW → 12 kW) directly addresses the white-space content shift in Phases 1-3
- DG Matrix supply relationship = exposure to NVIDIA MGX SST ramp

## Risks

- Diversified revenue dilutes pure 800VDC datacenter exposure (auto and industrial dominate)
- EU-listed; ADR access via IFNNY
- Competition from Wolfspeed (10 kV SiC pure-play), STMicroelectronics, onsemi
- BBU module ramp depends on Phase 1-2 power rack adoption rate (sidecar TAM peaks $11B in 2028)
