# Goldman Sachs

Global investment bank. **Tracked here as an analyst shop** — their GS SUSTAIN research practice publishes the most influential public-facing US power-demand forecasts on the AI buildout.

## Why tracked as an analyst

Like [[Epoch AI]], [[SemiAnalysis]], and [[McKinsey]], we expect to re-ingest Goldman Sachs research repeatedly. Their utility-side AI work has uniquely high reach (cited by Epoch, McKinsey, CSIS, every hyperscaler IR deck, every utility company strategic plan).

## Notable AI infrastructure research

- **[[Goldman Sachs - Generational Growth (2024)|Generational Growth: AI, data centers and the coming US power demand surge]]** (April 2024) — the canonical primary source for the "AI is reshaping US power demand" story
  - Key figures: 47 GW US incremental generation, $50B utility capex, 15% DC power CAGR, 60/40 gas/renewable mix
- **GS SUSTAIN reports** — sustainability-angle analysis of the AI buildout (related but distinct from the core utility research)
- **Powering the AI Era** — quarterly themed insights
- **AI capex tracking** — recurring updates on hyperscaler/sovereign capex announcements

## Beyond research — also a deal participant

- Investment banking on AI infra deals (advisory mandates, debt issuance, capital raising)
- Lending to data center / power asset projects via Goldman Sachs Asset Management infra funds
- Not currently a major equity stake in any tier-1 AI infra name to our knowledge — primarily a flow business

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

- **Power-side methodology anchor.** When other sources disagree on US power demand or generation mix, Goldman's framework is usually the right place to triangulate (and most other primaries cite back to it)
- **Bias toward conservative forecasts.** Goldman's 47 GW is *less* aggressive than McKinsey's implied US-share-of-156 GW; informative as a lower-bound check
- **Tradeable signal generator.** Their "Powering the AI Era" coverage frequently surfaces specific utility/IPP names ahead of broader market awareness

## What to watch

- Updates to the Generational Growth thesis (typically annual refresh)
- Their hyperscaler capex tracking pieces
- Any change in the 60/40 gas-renewable mix assumption — would shift exposure between [[GE Vernova]] (gas) and renewables names
