## Backlog & forward visibility

- **Year-end 2025 backlog: €38.8B** — ~4 years of forward revenue visibility
- Q4 2025 net bookings: **€13.2B** (of which **€7.4B EUV**)

## Forward guidance

- **FY2026: €36–40B revenue** (narrowed and raised)
- FY2026 gross margin: 51–53%
- Revenue weighted to **H2 2026**
- **2030 guide: €44–60B revenue** as High-NA EUV ramps + HBM adds a second cycle leg

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **The most concentrated supplier in the entire AI supply chain.** [[TSMC]] is sole leading-edge foundry; [[SK Hynix]] is dominant HBM; **ASML is sole EUV — period.** No alternative exists at any price.
2. **High-NA EUV is ramping** (2 systems shipped Q1 2026). This is the next-generation lithography that enables sub-2nm nodes. Without High-NA, TSMC N2 and beyond hit physical limits.
3. **€38.8B backlog** = forward visibility into 2028+ revenue. The buildout is already booked, not speculative.
4. **HBM as "second cycle leg"** validates [[HBM allocation]] story from the equipment side — memory fabs need lithography too, and ASML sees that as material into 2030.
5. **Picks-and-shovels exposure** to literally every advanced node tapeout. New [[ASML]] company page.
