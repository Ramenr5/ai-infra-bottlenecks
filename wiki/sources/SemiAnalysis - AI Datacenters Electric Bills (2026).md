# SemiAnalysis — Are AI Datacenters Increasing Electric Bills for American Households? (March 2026)

**Publisher:** [[SemiAnalysis]]
**Published:** 2026-03-03
**URL:** [newsletter.semianalysis.com/p/are-ai-datacenters-increasing-electric](https://newsletter.semianalysis.com/p/are-ai-datacenters-increasing-electric)

> [!success] Primary source — quantifies political/regulatory risk of DC power consumption
> Direct PJM Internal Market Monitor data. **PJM capacity prices spiked 9.3× (2024/25 → 2025/26).** Removing datacenters from PJM forecast would cut peak load 7,927 MW and **save $9.33B** (64% reduction in capacity payments). **PJM residents face ~15% bill increase** vs pre-AI-DC era. This is the regulatory backlash narrative quantified.

## PJM capacity market price spike

| Auction year | Price |
| --- | --- |
| 2024/25 | $29/MW-day |
| **2025/26** | **$270/MW-day (9.3× increase)** |
| **2026/27 & 2027/28** | **$329/MW-day (price-capped)** |

## Datacenter load impact on PJM

PJM Internal Market Monitor analysis:
- **Removing ALL datacenters: -7,927 MW peak load**
- = **$9.33B reduction in total capacity payments (64% cut)**
- Removing only future/non-operational datacenters: -4,654 MW → $7.74B savings (53% cut)

## Residential household impact

- $329/MW-day capacity = ~3.4¢/kWh
- Average PJM household: 880 kWh monthly
- **Additional monthly cost: $29.90 per household**
- **Annual PJM capacity auction total: ~$16B**
- **PJM region = 67 million residents facing ~15% bill increase** vs pre-AI-DC era

## PJM vs ERCOT comparison

| Market | Capacity price change |
| --- | --- |
| **PJM** | **9.3× spike (2024/25 → 2025/26)** |
| **ERCOT** | "Roughly stable for the last three years"; forward prices +11-17% |

ERCOT's market design (energy-only) absorbs datacenter growth differently than PJM's capacity-market design. **Texas datacenter buildout doesn't translate to residential bill spikes the way PJM does** — strategic advantage for Texas DC concentration.

## PJM forecast accuracy issues

- 2024 revision: 800 MW reduction from prior forecast
- 2025 revision: 1.1 GW reduction from 2024 forecast
- = PJM has been systematically over-forecasting datacenter load

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **Quantifies the political risk** to hyperscaler power expansion — 67M PJM residents paying $30/month more = real political pressure
2. **ERCOT design advantage** = Texas datacenter concentration is partly driven by this regulatory dynamic, not just power cost
3. **Capacity market spike** = direct beneficiaries are [[Constellation Energy]], [[Vistra]], [[Talen Energy]] who own existing generation that's now worth 10× more in capacity payments
4. **Regulatory backlash potential** = could force cost-shifting changes (data centers required to pay full capacity costs); affects [[Hyperscaler capex sustainability]] indirectly
5. **PJM over-forecasting** = supply may catch demand faster than headline numbers suggest
