## Data center / optical transceiver segment

- **Data Center revenue: +13% QoQ, +37% YoY**
- Growth drivers: **transceivers + optical circuit switches (OCS)**
- **800G and 1.6T transceivers** are key revenue drivers
- **1.6T ramping faster than expected**, better early-lifecycle margins
- 1.6T will be growth driver **through calendar 2026 and into 2027**

## Indium Phosphide capacity ramp (supply story)

- **6-inch indium phosphide produces >4× devices at <half the cost** vs 3-inch
- First transceivers containing 6-inch components **shipped this quarter**
- Internal indium phosphide capacity **to double by end of calendar 2026** (one quarter ahead of plan)

## Why it matters

1. **800G/1.6T transceivers are the bandwidth backbone** of scale-out AI networking. Every NVIDIA NVL72/NVL144 rack-to-rack link uses optical transceivers — Coherent + [[Lumentum]] + Fabrinet are the supply chain.
2. **NVIDIA equity backing** = strategic vendor lock-in. Coherent's roadmap is co-developed with NVIDIA's networking architecture (Spectrum-X, NVLink Switch).
3. **Indium phosphide capacity expansion** = upstream supply lever; 4× device output at half the cost = capacity-relief signal for the optical transceiver market
4. **Marvell + Broadcom supply the DSPs** that go INSIDE these transceivers — three layers of pick-and-shovel exposure (optical components, DSPs, networking switches)
