# Apollo — AI Capex Risk Outlook (2026)

**Publisher:** Apollo Academy (Apollo Global Management research; Chief Economist Torsten Slok)
**URLs:** [Top 5 Risks in 2026](https://www.apolloacademy.com/top-5-risks-in-2026/) · [Hyperscaler capex in perspective (PDF)](https://www.apolloacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Hyperscaler-capex-022226_v2.pdf) · [2026 Credit Outlook (PDF)](https://www.apolloacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Apollo-Global-2026-Credit-Outlook.pdf)

## Headline framing

The **institutional, credible bear/caution voice** (vs. polemical takes). Slok flags an AI-bubble burst as a **top-5 risk for 2026** and quantifies the scale: hyperscaler capex ≈ **2% of US GDP**, with the financing model shifting from self-funded to debt-funded — a setup that transmits any AI-demand disappointment straight into credit markets.

## Key numbers

| Metric | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Hyperscaler capex 2026** | **~$646B** (~2% of US GDP) |
| **Cumulative AI-related spend 2025-2029** | **~$2.7 trillion** |
| Capex growth since 2023 | **Tripled** |
| **Hyperscaler bond issuance Q4 2025** | **~$90B** (Oracle, Meta, Google, Amazon) |
| 2026 fixed-income supply | Rising from gov deficits + hyperscaler issuance → upward pressure on rates + spreads |

## The bear mechanism (Apollo framing)

1. **Self-funded → financing event**: capex outgrew operating cash flow; hyperscalers turning to debt
2. **Circular financing**: datacenters booked as long-term revenue even when subsidized by the hyperscaler or VC-funded — stimulating more capex without proven end-demand
3. **Credit transmission**: $90B/quarter issuance + government deficits widen spreads; an AI stumble hits Mag-7 equity AND IG credit simultaneously
4. **Trigger**: AI bubble burst → Mag-7 correction → capex slowdown → high-end consumer spending slowdown
