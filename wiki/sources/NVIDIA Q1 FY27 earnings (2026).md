## Supply commitments (THE critical CoWoS/HBM anchor number)

> CFO Colette Kress: "We remain front footed in securing sufficient supply to support our customers' growth. In Q1, we increased total supply inclusive of inventory purchase commitments on prepaids to **$145 billion**."

This is the canonical "NVIDIA prepayment to TSMC and SK Hynix" figure that drives the [[CoWoS packaging capacity]] and [[HBM allocation]] bottleneck dynamics. Concrete evidence that NVIDIA is locking up multi-year forward capacity.

## $1 trillion guidance through calendar 2027

> Jensen Huang: "giving us full confidence in **$1 trillion in Blackwell and Rubin revenue** we foresee from 2025 through calendar 2027"

Excludes: standalone Vera CPUs (~$20B expected), LPX, CPX products.

This is the single largest forward revenue guidance ever given by a US tech company, and it directly anchors hyperscaler procurement budgets.

## Vera Rubin roadmap

- Production shipments commence **Q3 FY27 (second half of calendar 2026)**
- Architecture: 7 purpose-built chips across 5 accelerated racks
- **35× higher inference throughput vs Blackwell**
- **10× greater AI factory revenue per dollar vs Blackwell**
- Vera CPU: 1.5× perf/core, 2× perf/watt, 4× density/rack vs x86
