## Headline figures

| **Generation mix for the new capacity** | **~60% natural gas / ~40% renewables** |

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **Verifies Epoch's secondary cites** — every Goldman-derived figure in [[Power & energy]] previously flowed through Epoch's citation. Now load-bearing primary.
2. **The 60/40 gas-renewables mix is the cleanest forward-looking number for generator-OEM exposure.** Gas dominates the marginal generation addition → [[GE Vernova]], [[Siemens Energy]], [[Mitsubishi Heavy Industries]] are the supply-side beneficiaries; renewables ($/MW lower for gas at the moment) capture the rest.
3. **$50B utility capex is modest vs the implied buildout** — Goldman is signaling that the dollar figure understates the buildout because most generation will be funded behind-the-meter or via PPAs rather than utility ratebase.
4. **47 GW incremental capacity through 2030** sits between Epoch's 30 GW (US AI DC capacity) and McKinsey's 156 GW (global AI DC demand). The disconnect is informative — Goldman counts US generation needed for DC load broadly (AI + non-AI); McKinsey counts global AI-specific capacity demand.
