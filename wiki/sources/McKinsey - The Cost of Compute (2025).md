# McKinsey — The Cost of Compute (2025)

**Publisher:** [[McKinsey & Company]]
**Published:** April 2025
**URL:** [mckinsey.com — The cost of compute](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/the-cost-of-compute-a-7-trillion-dollar-race-to-scale-data-centers)

> [!success] Primary source
> McKinsey TMT practice flagship on AI infrastructure capex. The **$5.2T / $7.9T figures** flowing through [[Situational Awareness, Two Years Later (2026)|Ansari]] as secondary attribution come from this report. Now load-bearing primary.

## Headline figures

| Scenario | DC capex through 2030 | AI capacity demand (incremental) | Total AI capacity (2030) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Base ("rising")** | **$5.2T AI + $1.5T traditional = $6.7T total** | 125 GW | 156 GW |
| **Accelerated** | **$7.9T** | 205 GW | — |

Per-GW implied build cost (base scenario): **~$42M per MW** of new AI DC capacity, all-in (chips, real estate/shell, mechanical/electrical/cooling, power, networking).

## Methodology notes

- **Demand-driven model:** starts with projected token-generation and training compute demand, derives required GW capacity, prices by capex category
- Two scenarios (base "rising" vs. "accelerated") differ on AI adoption pace
- Includes silicon (chips, packaging, memory), real estate, MEP (mechanical/electrical/plumbing), networking, and power infrastructure

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **Anchors the TAM.** $6.7T total DC capex through 2030 = the upper-bound investable opportunity set. AI-specific = $5.2T.
2. **Verifies the 156 GW 2030 number** that flows through every downstream source.
3. **Per-MW build cost ($42M)** is a useful benchmark for evaluating specific projects (e.g. [[Stargate]] $500B / 10 GW = $50M/MW; consistent with McKinsey's base scenario at the higher end).
4. **The skeptical voice matters too.** Per third-party coverage (IT Brew May 2025), several analysts question whether the $7T scenario is *fundable* given power/chip/capital constraints — i.e. McKinsey is sizing the demand side without solving for the supply side.
