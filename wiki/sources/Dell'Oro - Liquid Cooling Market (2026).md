# Dell'Oro — Data Center Liquid Cooling Market (2026)

**Publisher:** Dell'Oro Group (market-research firm; datacenter capex/networking/physical-infra data)
**URL:** [delloro.com/news/data-center-liquid-cooling-market-to-approach-7-billion-by-2029](https://www.delloro.com/news/data-center-liquid-cooling-market-to-approach-7-billion-by-2029-as-ai-deployments-accelerate/)

## Headline framing

Liquid cooling transitions from niche to foundational AI-infra requirement. Market roughly **doubles in 2025**, approaching **$7B by 2029** (some Dell'Oro releases cite $15B over a 5-yr horizon).

## Key numbers

| Metric | Value |
| --- | --- |
| 2025 market | ~$3B (roughly doubling) |
| **2029 market** | **~$7B** manufacturer revenue |
| 5-yr horizon (separate release) | **>$15B** |
| **GPU TDP driver** | **>4,000 W by 2029** |
| Dominant architecture | Single-phase DLC (vast majority of capacity) |
| Emerging | Two-phase DLC (accelerates when TDP exceeds single-phase limits); immersion (selective) |

## Vendor leaderboard (Dell'Oro)

- **[[Vertiv Holdings|Vertiv]]** — leading the liquid-cooling market
- **[[CoolIT Systems|CoolIT]], [[nVent Electric|nVent]], [[Boyd]]** — strong share positions
- **[[Aaon]]** — rapid growth via deep hyperscaler partnerships + customized solutions

## Contrarian flag (Uptime Institute)

Uptime Institute argues **liquid cooling "will not outgrow its high-density niche"** — air/hybrid persists for the large installed base of <50 kW racks. Useful counter to the all-liquid narrative.
