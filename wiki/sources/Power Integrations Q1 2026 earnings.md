# Power Integrations Q1 2026 earnings

**Publisher:** POWI IR (release) + Motley Fool (transcript)
**Date:** May 9, 2026 (call)
**URL:** [fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/05/09/powi-q1-2026-earnings-transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/05/09/powi-q1-2026-earnings-transcript/)
**Speakers:** Jennifer Lloyd (CEO), Nancy Erba (CFO)

## Headline framing

POWI is positioning itself as a **GaN silicon supplier to the 800VDC AI datacenter transition** with disclosed NVIDIA collaboration. Q1 2026 revenue +3% YoY ($108.3M); industrial +23% YoY; explicit NVIDIA collaboration on 1250V/1700V GaN; "data center SAM, including rack and grid applications, will exceed $1B by 2030."

## NVIDIA / 800VDC datacenter content (direct quotes)

> "Our ongoing collaboration with NVIDIA includes a variety of sockets utilizing our 1,250- and 1,700-volt GaN technologies in forthcoming 800-volt DC architectures."

> "We are engaging across the whole data center ecosystem — hyperscalers, server OEMs, rack providers, and power-supply providers."

> "The majority of future data center market opportunity is expected to be GaN-based."

> "Data center SAM, including rack and grid applications, will exceed $1 billion by 2030."

> POWI products operate natively "at 800, 1,200, 1,250, and 1,700 volts."

## FY26 outlook

- **Capex**: 5-6% of revenue, heavier in second half
- **Automotive revenue target**: $100M by 2029 (reiterated)
- **EV exposure**: in production or design with **17 of top 20 EV manufacturers**; ~$100/vehicle content target over "next several years"

## Why it matters

- **First clean primary signal** that POWI is a credible 800VDC AI datacenter silicon supplier — not just a consumer power play
- NVIDIA collaboration is the **single highest-credibility validation** an integrated-switcher vendor can have in 2026
- $1B 2030 datacenter SAM is the management-disclosed TAM; pairs with [[SemiAnalysis - Inside the 800VDC Revolution (2026)]] sidecar TAM of $11B 2028 peak and SST TAM $13B 2030 — POWI claims ~5-8% silicon take-rate on $13-24B equipment market
- Margin pressure (EPS -19% YoY) means **the datacenter ramp is still pre-revenue** in volume terms
