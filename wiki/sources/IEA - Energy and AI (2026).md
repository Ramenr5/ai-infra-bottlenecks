# IEA — Energy and AI

**Publisher:** International Energy Agency (authoritative intergovernmental energy body)
**URL:** [iea.org/reports/energy-and-ai/energy-demand-from-ai](https://www.iea.org/reports/energy-and-ai/energy-demand-from-ai)

## Headline framing

The canonical *global* datacenter electricity-demand anchor — complements the wiki's US-centric power sources ([[Goldman Sachs - Generational Growth (2024)]], [[Can AI scaling continue through 2030 (2024)|Epoch]], SemiAnalysis). Global DC demand **~415 TWh (2024) → ~945 TWh (2030)**, ~15%/yr, with AI ("accelerated servers") the fast component at **30%/yr**.

## Key numbers

| Metric | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Global DC electricity 2024** | **~415 TWh** (~1.5% of global) |
| **Global DC electricity 2030** | **~945 TWh** (~3% of global) |
| Growth rate 2024-2030 | **~15%/yr** |
| Accelerated (AI) servers | **+30%/yr** |
| Conventional servers | +9%/yr |

### Regional (2024 → 2030)

| Region | Increase | % rise |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **United States** | **+240 TWh** | **+130%** (per-capita 540 → >1,200 kWh) |
| China | +175 TWh | +170% |
| Europe | +45 TWh | +70% |
| Japan | +15 TWh | +80% |
| Southeast Asia | more than doubles | — |

### 2035 sensitivity cases

| Scenario | 2035 demand | % of global |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Lift-Off** | **~1,700 TWh** | 4.4% |
| **High Efficiency** | ~970 TWh | 2.6% |
| **Headwinds** | ~700 TWh | <2% |

## Constraint findings

- "The broader energy system requires longer lead times" — the core speed-mismatch thesis: AI moves faster than grid/generation can be built
- "Substantial uncertainty" in demand → the 1,000 TWh spread between Lift-Off and Headwinds by 2035 IS the forecast risk
