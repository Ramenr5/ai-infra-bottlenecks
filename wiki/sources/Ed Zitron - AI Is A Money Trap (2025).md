# Ed Zitron — AI Is A Money Trap

**Publisher:** Ed Zitron, "Where's Your Ed At" (newsletter) · Published 2025-08-06

> [!warning] Polemical source — steelman, not gospel
> Zitron is the most aggressive AI bear; the piece is argumentative and predates the Anthropic/Bedrock profitability inflection ([[SemiAnalysis - Anthropic Bedrock AWS Margins (2026)]], May 2026) that partially rebuts it. Recorded as the steelman bear case to stress-test the wiki's bull lean. Treat figures as the author's framing.

## Central thesis

GenAI cannot generate revenue to justify its capex + opex; the sector runs on continuous VC/capex infusions with no profitable exits, and the datacenter buildout (~50% of US economic growth in H1 2025, per Zitron) rests on unprofitable operations that will contract when external funding rationalizes.

## Key bear arguments + figures

| Argument | Figure / claim |
| --- | --- |
| **Capex vs revenue gap** | ~$300-520B annual AI capex (2025) vs ~$20B 2025 GenAI revenue; ~$35-40B cumulative GenAI revenue 2024-25 |
| AI capex as macro | 1.2% of US GDP H1 2025; ~50% of US economic growth |
| **OpenAI economics** | $500B rumored valuation; ~$12-13B annualized rev; raised $18.3B in 2 months |
| Oracle/Crusoe/OpenAI | OpenAI to pay Oracle ~$30B/yr from 2028 vs ~$12.7B 2025 revenue — "customer losing ~$10B/yr can't pay $30B/yr" |
| FCF degradation | Big-4 net income +73% to $91B (Q2 YoY) but **FCF −30% to $40B** — diverging since 2023 on capex |
| Microsoft signal | "Basically no leases in last 12 months"; committed DC construction down YoY |
| No exits | Inflection/Windsurf/Character = acqui-hires/licensing, not rational-valuation M&A |
| Revenue recognition | OpenAI/Anthropic ARR figures jump between non-calendar-month bases; concentration (Anthropic $1.4B of $5B from 2 customers) |

## The "$2T or wasted" math (widely cited)

Derived elsewhere from similar logic: big tech needs **~$2 trillion in AI revenue by 2030** to justify the cumulative datacenter capex — or the spend was value-destructive.

- [[SemiAnalysis - Anthropic Bedrock AWS Margins (2026)]] (May 2026): Anthropic inference GM **mid-60s**, **OI-profitable in 2Q26**, $30B ARR (+$21B net new in Q1) — the "no profitable GenAI" claim is weakening at the frontier-lab level
