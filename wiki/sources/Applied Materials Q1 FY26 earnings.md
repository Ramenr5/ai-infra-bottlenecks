## CEO Gary Dickerson — the headline call

> "Global semiconductor industry revenues could reach **$1 trillion in 2026**, **several years earlier than previously predicted**."

This is **a major upgrade to the industry growth trajectory**. Prior consensus pointed at $1T in 2028-2030 window.

## 2026 outlook for AMAT

- **AMAT's own semiconductor business: >20% growth in 2026**
- Tracking **higher levels of planned capex from cloud service providers**
- Three fastest-growing markets: leading-edge logic, HBM DRAM, advanced packaging

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **AMAT's record DRAM revenue confirms the [[HBM allocation]] supply-side story.** Memory fabs are buying tools as fast as AMAT can ship them — direct evidence that [[SK Hynix]]'s M15X expansion and Samsung/Micron analogs are in active capex deployment.
2. **"$1T industry 2026, several years early"** materially upgrades the trajectory framing for [[Capex trajectory thesis]] — independent CEO-level confirmation of acceleration.
3. **Foundry/logic 62% segment share** = TSMC, Samsung, Intel all in active capex absorption. Validates the $52-56B TSMC capex guide via the equipment-side mirror.
4. **Hyperscaler capex absorption confirmed at equipment level** — AMAT sees increased orders downstream from cloud customers' capex announcements
5. **Advanced packaging** is a named fastest-growing market — corroborates [[CoWoS packaging capacity]] severity. AMAT supplies tools that go into TSMC's CoWoS line.
