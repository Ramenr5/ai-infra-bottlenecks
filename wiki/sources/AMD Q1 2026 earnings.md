## Data Center segment (the AI story)

- **DC revenue: $5.8B (+57% YoY)** — primary engine
- Drivers:
  - **5th gen EPYC** server CPUs (taking share from Intel Xeon)
  - **Instinct MI350 series GPUs** (AI accelerators)
- MI355X MLPerf: strong competitive performance; leadership in multiple categories

## The Meta deal — major

- **Meta plans to deploy up to 6 GW of AMD Instinct GPUs**
- **First 1-GW deployment powered by custom AMD Instinct MI450**
- Comparable in scope to [[Broadcom]]-Google TPU or [[Broadcom]]-Meta MTIA partnerships
- = AMD landed a major hyperscaler custom-silicon win on Instinct platform

## Helios (rack-scale AI factory)

- Initial volume Q3 2026
- Significant ramp Q4 2026 → Q1 2027
- AMD's answer to NVIDIA's NVL72 rack-scale architecture

## Forward roadmap

- **MI400 series** — Helios reference platform
- **MI450 custom** — Meta deal anchor; H2 2026 ramp
- **$120B server CPU income forecast by 2030**

## Lisa Su quotes

> "A clear inflection in our growth trajectory and a structural shift in our business"

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **AMD as #2 in AI accelerators with material momentum.** $5.8B DC revenue +57% YoY is the strongest growth among public AI silicon vendors. Combined with NVIDIA's $75B DC revenue, the AI accelerator market is much larger than just NVIDIA suggests.
2. **Meta 6 GW Instinct commitment** = the largest hyperscaler commitment to non-NVIDIA silicon publicly disclosed. Comparable in scope to [[Stargate]] from a single-customer perspective.
3. **Custom MI450 for Meta** = AMD doing hyperscaler custom silicon (similar model to Broadcom's TPU/MTIA work). Different business model than catalog Instinct sales.
4. **Helios rack-scale** = direct competitor to NVIDIA NVL72; validates rack-scale as the unit of AI infrastructure deployment
5. **5th gen EPYC taking share from Intel Xeon** = secondary picks-and-shovels exposure to server CPU upgrade cycle (drives [[Power & energy]] demand among other things)
