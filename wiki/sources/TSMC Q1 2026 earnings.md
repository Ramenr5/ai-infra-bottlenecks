## CoWoS — the critical bottleneck quote

> CEO C.C. Wei: **"Our CoWoS capacity is very tight and remains sold out through 2025 and into 2026"**

Plus, per Silicon Analysts' foundry-allocation tracker:
- **CoWoS demand exceeds supply by 40–50%**
- **CoWoS lead times: 50+ weeks**
- N3 (3nm): fully booked
- N7: available
- Supply constraints expected to persist into 2027

## AI wafer demand

- **11× growth in AI wafer demand 2022–2026** per TSMC's own forecast (cited via TrendForce)
- Advanced-node wafer demand "about three times" greater than available supply per TSMC's own statement

## CoWoS roadmap (forward-looking)

- **2028:** 14-reticle-size CoWoS supporting **20 HBM stacks**
- **2029:** Beyond-14-reticle CoWoS supporting **24 HBM stacks**

For context: current Blackwell B200 uses 8 HBM stacks; the path to 20–24 stacks per accelerator represents a 2.5–3× per-chip memory expansion, materially supporting the [[Data wall|larger model + longer context]] trajectory.

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **CoWoS lead time of 50+ weeks** is *much worse* than [[Can AI scaling continue through 2030 (2024)|Epoch 2024]]'s framing. TSMC's 60%/yr planned growth is not keeping pace with NVIDIA's $145B supply commitments — see [[NVIDIA Q1 FY27 earnings (2026)]].
2. **40–50% demand gap** = severity acute, near-term unresolvable. Updates [[CoWoS packaging capacity]] from "acute" to deeply binding.
3. **HPC = 61% of TSMC revenue** confirms how much of TSMC's business is now downstream of AI. TSMC is effectively bet on AI now.
4. **FY26 capex $52–56B** is ~2× the FY2023 capex run-rate, validating the [[CoWoS packaging capacity|CoWoS expansion]] trajectory and providing concrete capex absorption for [[Capital flows & deal structures]].
5. **2028 → 24 HBM stacks per chip** path provides multi-year visibility on memory-per-accelerator scaling — directly supports the [[HBM allocation]] supplier rent capture thesis.
