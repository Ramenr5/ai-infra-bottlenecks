# P3 Institute — From Open Source Software to Open Weights

**Publisher:** P3 Institute (Substack) · Author: attributed to **Bill Gurley** (verify) · ~2026-05-09

> Value is the *framework + strategic read*, not new numbers. Surfaced as the kind of high-signal essay the AI Narrative Scout exists to catch.

## Core thesis

Open source has evolved from a development methodology into a **deliberate corporate strategy** — used to neutralize stronger competitors, commoditize expensive inputs, and deny any single vendor pricing power. In AI, "open source" really means **open *weights*** (downloadable parameters), and the open-vs-closed outcome will determine **where AI value and power concentrate**.

## The framework — open source as a competitive weapon (6 historical plays)

| Play | Year | Actor | Target | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Android | 2007 | Google | Apple mobile monopoly | 73% global handset OS |
| Open Compute Project | 2011 | Meta | Hardware vendor margins | ~$295B infra mkt (2029) |
| Kubernetes | 2014 | Google | AWS cloud lock-in | 82% enterprise adoption |
| LF Networking | 2017 | Carriers | Cisco margins | 70% of mobile subs |
| RISC-V | 2010 | UC Berkeley | ARM/Intel IP | 25% silicon penetration |
| Overture Maps | 2022 | AWS/Meta/MSFT | Google maps moat | production 2024 |

**Success conditions:** (a) a funded coalition for the alternative, (b) a neutral foundation preventing recapture, (c) regulation that doesn't outlaw competition.

## The AI map: closed "cathedral" vs open weights

- **Closed (cathedral):** [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]] (US, closed weights); [[Meta]] withdrew from the open frontier (closed "Muse Spark," Apr 2026)
- **Open-weight leaders — China dominant:** DeepSeek R1, Alibaba Qwen, Moonshot Kimi, Zhipu GLM
- **Western open alt:** Mistral (675B, Apache-2.0, **$20M → $400M ARR in a year**)
- **Hyperscaler hedges:** Google (Gemini closed + Gemma open-edge); Microsoft (OpenAI + Azure open distribution); Amazon "curiously absent"

## Key data points

- Open source already: 55% of OS, 49% cloud/containers, 45% databases, **40% of AI/ML workloads**
- China policy: **two consecutive five-year plans (2021-25, 2026-30) enshrine open source**; Mar 2025 — eight govt bodies mandate RISC-V in critical infra; RISC-V 4,600+ orgs, ~20B cores
- DeepSeek R1 (Jan 2025) tanked US AI stocks — the open-weights shock

## The prediction (the falsifiable claim)

> Absent a credible **Western open-weight frontier** player, **Chinese open models become the global default by 2030**, and the US ends up technologically isolated from the majority of the world's ~6B AI users.

## Why it matters for the thesis

**This is a direct challenge to the closed-lab value-capture thesis.** Much of the bull case ([[Capex trajectory thesis]], [[SemiAnalysis - Anthropic Bedrock AWS Margins (2026)]]) assumes a handful of US labs extract durable platform rents. If open weights commoditize the model layer:
- **Value migrates** from model labs → inference/tooling/distribution (commodity) and → whoever owns the cheapest compute + the ecosystem
- **Compute demand may still rise** (open + cheap → Jevons → more inference), so the *infra* picks-and-shovels thesis can survive even if the *lab-rent* thesis doesn't — an important distinction for [[AI infrastructure investment map (May 2026)]]
- **Geopolitical tail:** Chinese-default-models is a regime/policy risk not currently priced in the wiki
