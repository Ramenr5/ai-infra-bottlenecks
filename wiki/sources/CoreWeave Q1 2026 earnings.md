# CoreWeave Q1 2026 earnings

**Publisher:** CoreWeave IR + SEC 8-K; corroborated by CNBC / Motley Fool
**Date:** reported 2026-05-07
**URLs:** [investors.coreweave.com Q1 2026 release](https://investors.coreweave.com/news/news-details/2026/CoreWeave-Reports-Strong-First-Quarter-2026-Results/) · [SEC 8-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1769628/000176962826000220/coreweave1q26earningspress.htm) · [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/07/coreweave-crwv-q1-earnings-report-2026.html)

## Headline

The largest pure-play neocloud's Q1 2026: revenue ~$2.1B (+112% YoY), a **$99.4B revenue backlog**, and **3.5 GW total contracted power** — concrete confirmation of how much forward AI-compute demand is locked into take-or-pay contracts.

## Key numbers (Q1 2026)

| Metric | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Revenue** | **~$2.1B** (+32% QoQ, +112% YoY) |
| **Revenue backlog** | **$99.4B** |
| New commitments signed in Q1 | **>$40B** |
| **Total contracted power** | **3.5 GW** |

## Why it matters

- The **$99.4B backlog vs $12-13B 2026 revenue** = the clearest single measure of contracted forward AI-compute demand (and the project-finance collateral underpinning [[AI debt financing capacity]])
- 3.5 GW contracted / >1 GW active = neocloud power demand at hyperscaler scale
- Backlog growth (>$40B new in one quarter) is a bull signal for [[Capex trajectory thesis]]; the debt funding it is the bear signal for [[Capex bust scenario]]
