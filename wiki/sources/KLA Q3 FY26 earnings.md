## Semiconductor Process Control segment

- **$3.08B** (90.3% of total revenue) — +12.6% YoY, +3% sequential
- End-market mix: **62% foundry/logic, 38% memory** (systems basis)
- **Wafer inspection: $1.74B (51% of revenue)** — +16% YoY, +11% sequential
- **Service revenue: $775M (22.7%)** — +15.8% YoY (recurring, high-margin)

## Advanced packaging breakout (NEW)

- **+14pp market share gained** in Advanced Wafer-Level Packaging
- **~70% YoY revenue growth** in advanced packaging
- **~$1B advanced packaging business in FY26** (up from ~$635M in 2025)
- Direct exposure to [[CoWoS packaging capacity]] expansion at TSMC

## Why it matters

1. **Process control monopoly** — KLA has ~58% market share, no near peer. Every wafer must go through inspection/metrology multiple times to identify defects and validate process windows. This is **mandatory equipment** regardless of node/foundry.
2. **Advanced packaging $1B+ in FY26** = direct primary confirmation of [[CoWoS packaging capacity]] expansion. The packaging line at TSMC needs KLA tools at every step.
3. **Less cyclical than AMAT/LRCX** — service revenue 23%, mandatory process control = baseline demand that doesn't compress in downturns
4. **62/38 foundry-logic/memory split** mirrors industry capacity-add ratio
