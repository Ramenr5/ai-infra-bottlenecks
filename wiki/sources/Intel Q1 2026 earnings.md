## DCAI (Data Center + AI) segment

- **DCAI revenue: $5.1B** (+22% YoY, +7% sequential)
- **AI-specific revenue within DCAI: >$750M** (vs ~$400M YA)
- Drivers:
  - Rising ASPs on **Xeon 6 "Granite Rapids"** processors
  - **Gaudi 3 uptake** in AWS + IBM Cloud clusters
  - **Gaudi 4 captured ~6% data center accelerator market share** — value-per-dollar play

## Panther Lake / 18A node

- **Panther Lake (18A) volume ramping 6-7× QoQ** in Q2 2026
- Q2 gross margin steps down 41% → 39% on Panther Lake ramp
- **18A yields hitting year-end target by mid-year** per CFO Zinsner
- Panther Lake = Intel internal first product on 18A; launched Jan 2026
- 18A is Intel's first node leading vs TSMC (in feature density terms) after ~5 years of trailing

## Intel Foundry segment

- **Foundry revenue: $5.4B** (+16% YoY)
- **Operating loss narrowed: $2.51B Q4 2025 → $1.8B Q1 2026**
- First foundry customer products on 18A starting to ramp

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **TSMC alternative at the foundry level — real but small.** Intel Foundry at $5.4B revenue is tiny vs TSMC's $35.9B/quarter, but the operating loss is narrowing and 18A is real product. Adds optionality to the foundry concentration story.
2. **US-headquartered IDM** — only US company manufacturing leading-edge logic at any scale. CHIPS Act exposure + supply chain resilience play.
3. **Gaudi 4 = 6% accelerator market share** — small but non-zero AI accelerator footprint, providing some alternative to NVIDIA's dominance (alongside AMD).
4. **AI businesses = 60% of total revenue** = Intel has become an AI company by revenue mix, comparable to AMD's transformation
5. **18A is the inflection node** — if Intel Foundry wins meaningful external customers on 18A or 18AP, the [[Lithography monopoly]] and [[CoWoS packaging capacity]] dynamics shift; if 18A flops, Intel's foundry ambitions stall and TSMC concentration deepens
