**Publisher:** [[SemiAnalysis]]

> [!success] Primary source — Oracle's "GPU landlord" thesis explained
> Oracle leased **>2 GW of datacenter capacity from Nov 2023 to Jan 2025** — single largest US datacenter lessor in that period. **ByteDance is Oracle's largest GPU customer** (not OpenAI). Johor, Malaysia emerges as world's 2nd largest AI hub via Oracle/ByteDance.

## Datacenter capacity & leasing

- **>2 GW leased Nov 2023 → Jan 2025** = single largest US DC lessor in that period
- Abilene, TX campus: 220 MW initial (mid-2024) → 660 MW (early 2025) → **~880 MW OpenAI deal**
- **ByteDance APAC cluster: 600-700 MW within a year; potentially 2 GW by 2028**

## Financial figures

- 2 GW leased = ~$3B annual expense
- Abilene contract value: ~$15-20B
- **OpenAI five-year contracts: ~$10B annual expense to OpenAI**
- Larry Ellison forecast: **>$130B of contracts to be booked in next 12 months**
- Oracle targeted: **>100% RPO growth FY26** + **>$30B capex** (later raised to $50B per Q3 FY26)
- OpenAI generating ~$2B annualized revenue mid-2024

## Customer relationships

### ByteDance — Oracle's largest GPU customer
- Characterized as "one of the world's largest and fastest-growing users of GPUs, of comparable scale to US hyperscalers"
- **Oracle's largest GPU customer today** (June 2025 framing; OpenAI overtook later)
- Concentration risk dimension separate from OpenAI

### OpenAI
- Primary focus; early 2025 = second contract phase booking
- Continues to scale (see [[Oracle Q3 FY26 earnings]] $300B / 4.5 GW)
