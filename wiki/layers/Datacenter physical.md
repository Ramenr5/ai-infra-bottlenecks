# Datacenter physical

The "building the box" layer: site selection, permitting, construction (shell + MEP), liquid cooling, networking / optical interconnect, and the skilled workforce that installs it all. As rack density climbs past air-cooling limits (~30–50 kW/rack → 100+ kW for Blackwell, future generations higher), several previously cheap components become acute chokepoints.

## Sub-domains

- **Cooling** — [[Direct-to-chip liquid cooling]] (D2C), [[Immersion cooling]], [[CDUs]] (coolant distribution units), [[Rear-door heat exchangers]]
- **Water & thermal** — [[Datacenter water permits]], evaporative cooling, dry cooling, water reuse
- **Networking & optics** — [[Optical transceivers]] (800G → 1.6T), [[Switch ASICs]], [[Co-packaged optics]], [[Fiber buildout]]
- **Real estate & construction** — site selection, [[Datacenter permitting]], shell construction, [[EPC contractors]]
- **Workforce** — [[Electrician shortage]], [[Datacenter technician labor]], [[Construction labor]]

## Bottlenecks (to be populated as sources land)

- [[Liquid cooling component supply]] — CDU, manifolds, quick-disconnect fittings; ramping from niche to mainstream
- [[Optical transceiver supply]] — InP / VCSEL capacity, advanced packaging
- [[Datacenter water permits]] — increasingly contested in arid regions
- [[Electrician shortage]] — IBEW journeyman pipeline + community college throughput
- [[Permitting and zoning]] — Loudoun moratorium debates, Dublin/Frankfurt local pushback

## Where the money flows

- Hyperscaler self-build vs. colocation lease — split is shifting toward owned campuses for largest hyperscalers
- REIT capex: [[Equinix]], [[Digital Realty]] expanding hyperscale + retail
- Neocloud capex: [[CoreWeave]], [[Crusoe]], [[Lambda]] building dedicated GPU campuses, often debt-financed against contracted hyperscaler offtake
- Construction firms: [[Quanta Services]], [[Jacobs]], [[AECOM]], [[Fluor]] booking record backlogs
