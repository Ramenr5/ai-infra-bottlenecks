# Oklahoma

Emerging US AI datacenter geography. Cited in our wiki via [[Cerebras Systems]]'s [[Cerebras Oklahoma datacenter|main operating datacenter in Oklahoma City]].

## Why it's on the map

- Climate + land availability favorable for large industrial facilities
- **SPP grid** (Southwest Power Pool) — distinct from PJM (Northern Virginia) and ERCOT (Texas); gas-heavy generation mix with substantial wind in the western portions
- Tax/permitting environment broadly business-friendly
- Lower data center density historically vs. NoVA/Texas — less competitive pressure on land and power

## Current AI infra footprint

- [[Cerebras Oklahoma datacenter]] — Cerebras's flagship operating facility; **6,000-ton chiller plant** producing 5°C chilled water (atypically cold inlet requirements for WSE-3 thermal design)

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

- **Geographic diversification** away from saturated NoVA / contested Loudoun and increasingly-crowded ERCOT
- **SPP grid** has had less AI-driven demand pressure to date; transmission queue depth less congested than PJM
- Single-anchor (Cerebras) so far — but if more AI infra lands in SPP, secondary effects on Oklahoma grid planning, gas-turbine supply, water rights
