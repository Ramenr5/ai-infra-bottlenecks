# Johor, Malaysia

**The world's second-largest AI hub** (per [[SemiAnalysis - Oracle Winning AI Compute (2025)]]) — emerged via ByteDance partnership with [[Oracle]]. Part of the broader **Singapore-Johor-Batam** regional cluster.

## Why it's on the map

- ByteDance is **[[Oracle]]'s largest GPU customer** (June 2025 framing); compute concentrated in Johor
- Singapore is the historical SE Asia datacenter hub but power-constrained — Johor (just across the strait in Malaysia) provides land + power + permitting relief
- Singapore datacenter moratorium pushed expansion to Johor
- Lower power costs vs Singapore; favorable Malaysian government posture toward DC investment

## Capacity / commitments

- **ByteDance APAC cluster: 600-700 MW within a year of June 2025 framing; potentially 2 GW by 2028**
- Concentrated across Oracle facilities + other operators
- Comparable in scale to a major US AI infra geography

## Why it matters for the bottleneck thesis

1. **Non-US compute concentration at scale** — first wiki entry of a hyperscaler-scale AI geography outside the US/EU/Korea/Taiwan/Japan axis
2. **ByteDance compute consumption is comparable to US hyperscalers** — material demand that doesn't show up in our US-focused Big Five framing
3. **Power/permitting arbitrage from Singapore to Johor** = template for other regions; Malaysia gaining structural AI infra share
4. **Geopolitical positioning** — Malaysia is more neutral than Singapore on US-China tech tensions; ByteDance can deploy without geopolitical friction

## Counterparties

- **[[Oracle]]** = primary operator; largest US-headquartered footprint
- **ByteDance** = anchor customer (TikTok parent; massive AI compute consumer)
- Local datacenter operators: YTL Power, NTT, Equinix (Johor footprint)
- Singapore neighbors: Equinix, Digital Realty, Singtel

## Open questions

- Total operational MW vs 2 GW 2028 target
- Other anchor tenants beyond ByteDance
- Malaysian government incentives + tax regime
- Power source mix
- Geopolitical risk if US-China tensions escalate

## What to watch

- ByteDance capacity additions
- Other hyperscalers expanding in Johor (Microsoft, Google, AWS)
- YTL Power and Malaysian utility capacity additions
- Cross-border (Singapore-Johor) interconnect capacity
