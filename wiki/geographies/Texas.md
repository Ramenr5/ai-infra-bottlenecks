# Texas

The fastest-growing US destination for hyperscale AI infrastructure as of 2026. Likely to overtake [[Northern Virginia]] as the largest US AI-load geography this decade.

## Why Texas

- **[[ERCOT]] grid** — relatively fast interconnect queue vs. PJM/CAISO; favorable for behind-the-meter generation
- **Abundant natural gas** — Permian-adjacent supply; gas-turbine power dispatchable on AI-build timelines
- **Permitting speed** — state and local permitting markedly faster than CA/VA equivalents
- **Land availability** — large parcels at low cost relative to coastal markets
- **Tax environment** — favorable for capital-intensive industrial assets

## Assets concentrated here

- [[Stargate Texas]] — 20 datacenters, 500K sqft each, **10 GW total**
- Other hyperscaler builds (not enumerated in current sources — populate as ingest deepens)

## Risks / open questions

- **ERCOT capacity:** can the grid absorb 10+ GW of new firm AI load on top of baseline state growth without reliability events?
- **Water:** Texas drought risk vs. cooling demand for 10 GW thermal load
- **Permitting blowback:** the LoCo/PJM-style local pushback hasn't hit Texas yet; could it?
- **Gas-turbine supply:** Texas builds rely heavily on dispatchable gas. [[GE Vernova]] / [[Siemens Energy]] / [[Mitsubishi Heavy Industries]] backlogs apply.

## ERCOT design advantage (per [[SemiAnalysis - AI Datacenters Electric Bills (2026)]])

Critical strategic context: **ERCOT prices "roughly stable for last three years"** despite Stargate-scale buildout. Forward prices only +11-17% vs PJM's 9.3× spike.

ERCOT's energy-only market design absorbs datacenter growth differently than PJM's capacity-market design. **DCs don't translate to residential bill spikes in Texas the way they do in NoVA.** This is a major structural reason Texas wins AI infra share over [[Northern Virginia]] — not just power cost, but regulatory dynamics.

## Adjacent geographies

- [[Saudi Arabia]] — sovereign-backed competing buildout
