# PJM

The largest US grid operator (mid-Atlantic, 13 states + DC; 67M people) — and the epicenter of **AI-datacenter power-price backlash**. Home to [[Northern Virginia]], the world's densest datacenter cluster. PJM's capacity market is where AI load growth has most visibly spiked prices and triggered residential-bill politics.

## The capacity-price spike (per [[SemiAnalysis - AI Datacenters Electric Bills (2026)]])

| Delivery year | Capacity price |
| --- | --- |
| 2024/25 | $29/MW-day |
| **2025/26** | **$270/MW-day (9.3× spike)** |
| 2026/27 & 2027/28 | $329/MW-day (price-capped) |

**Residential impact:** 67M PJM residents face **~15% bill increase (~$29.90/month)** attributable to AI DC buildout. Removing all DCs would cut capacity payments $9.33B (−64%).

This is the **regulatory-backlash geography** — the political risk that AI infra growth provokes. Contrast with [[ERCOT]] (energy-only, prices "roughly stable") — the reason Texas is winning AI infra share over [[Northern Virginia]].

## Why it matters for the thesis

- **Capacity market = the mechanism** that converts AI load growth into consumer bill increases → political/regulatory risk to siting
- The PJM-vs-ERCOT contrast is the cleanest evidence that **market design (not just power cost) drives where AI infra lands**
- Interconnection queue multi-year backlog → drives [[onsite gas]] + behind-the-meter
